| T1 | At Arcline Enclosure-540, identify the exact coolant splash-guard segment that leaks or was reopened during service, and record whether the complaint is splash escape, standing coolant behind the guard, or a loose seal strip on the lower edge. Note the left or right side, the associated drain trough, and the nozzle bank that was running when the leak was first seen. |
| T2 | Remove only the affected guard section and expose guard flange, neoprene seal strip SG-L1, drain slot DR-S1, chip ledge, lower way-cover lip, and nearby coolant hose clip while preserving the wet track on metal surfaces. Keep upstream nozzle direction and downstream drain path visible together because the first wet point is often not the true leak origin. |
| T3 | Inspect the seal for compression set, edge cuts, chip packing, twisted corners, and pull-out from the retainer channel, then compare the suspect segment with the adjacent healthy guard section. A flattened lower lip, a bowed flange, or a blocked drain slot each creates a different boundary even though all three can leave coolant on the same floor area. |
| T4 | Run a controlled coolant test at the same nozzle program and flow condition that produced the complaint, and compare splash behavior at seal lip SG-L1, flange joint, and drain exit DR-S1. If coolant stays behind the guard until the drain slot overflows, keep the boundary on the drain path; if it escapes immediately at the flange, keep it on seal compression or flange flatness instead. |
| T5 | Correct only the failed seal strip, flange straightness, fastener clamp, or hose clip path, then reseat the guard from the upper screw line down to the lower drain edge so the seal compresses evenly along the full section. Confirm that the hose clip no longer pushes the guard outward and that the drain trough remains open from inlet to outlet. |
| T6 | Refit the surrounding panels and repeat the same coolant pattern while jogging the related axis through the original travel zone, because splash faults often return once vibration and guard movement are restored. Record the final dry line, the drain discharge, and any remaining residue after the test stops. |
| T7 | Close the guard repair only after the note names the exact failed boundary, such as lower seal strip SG-L1, bowed flange, blocked drain slot DR-S1, or hose-clip interference, and links it to the repeated coolant test result. The machine may return to service when the tested section stays dry on the outer face, drains freely on the inner face, and preserves seal compression after the axis motion cycle. |
