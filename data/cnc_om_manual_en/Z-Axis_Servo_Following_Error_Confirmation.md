| T1 | On Arcline VMC-805, record the Z-axis following-error complaint in terms of feed rate, direction, load, and alarm timing, because vertical-axis faults do not behave the same in rise, fall, and hold. Capture the actual alarm stage, the current counterbalance setting, and whether the problem appears under acceleration, steady feed, or position hold. |
| T2 | Check low-risk Z motion first and expose the ball screw, coupling, brake housing, counterbalance connection, and guide-truck area while preserving witness marks and lubrication film. Keep the mechanical load path and the brake or counterbalance path visible together because following error on Z usually sits at the intersection of those systems. |
| T3 | Reproduce the fault under the original motion profile and compare commanded position, actual position, and servo load or current if available. If error spikes during release or hold rather than during steady travel, keep the next split on brake drag or counterbalance imbalance instead of on guide friction alone. |
| T4 | Inspect ball-screw support, coupling clamp, guide-truck lubrication, brake behavior, and counterbalance response in the order they influence Z-axis load. Compare upper and lower witnesses separately so cover rub, dry guides, or a dragging brake are not collapsed into one vague mechanical resistance diagnosis. |
| T5 | Measure or observe the specific boundary suspected from the timing, such as brake release delay, counterbalance pressure variation, coupling slip, or guide drag, then compare the result with the machine target. This step keeps the correction tied to the real dominant load source instead of replacing parts across multiple Z subsystems. |
| T6 | Correct only the identified boundary and restore covers, lube points, cable supports, and hose supports to their production path before rerunning the profile. Confirm that the repaired component is seated or supported correctly and that no adjacent path now adds a new load into the vertical axis. |
| T7 | Repeat the same Z motion profile and compare following error, servo load, and mechanical behavior with the as-found condition. The repair is not accepted if the alarm disappears under one short move but returns once the axis repeats the original acceleration or hold sequence. |
| T8 | Close the work only after the report names the dominant cause boundary, such as brake drag, counterbalance imbalance, support friction, coupling slip, or guide lubrication loss, and ties it to the motion profile that proved the correction. Accept the repair when the same Z profile now runs without following error and the corrected hardware remains stable after the repeated test. |
