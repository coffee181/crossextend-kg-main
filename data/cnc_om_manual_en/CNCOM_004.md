| T1 | On Novaris VMC-760, record the spindle complaint as orientation fault at low speed, speed fluctuation near 6000 rpm, or encoder alarm that changes after warm-up. Note the exact speed window, whether the spindle reaches orientation repeatably, and whether cabinet vibration or recent motor-end service preceded the fault. |
| T2 | Isolate the machine safely and expose the spindle encoder body, the Oldham coupling hubs, the center insert, the upper bracket, the motor-end shaft, and the encoder cable clamp. Preserve the original clamp marks and hub positions because an encoder fault caused by hub walk is often invisible after the screws are disturbed. |
| T3 | Inspect the coupling for cracked center insert, axial hub shift, loose clamp screws, or witness marks that show one hub has moved along the shaft. Measure the coupling gap and compare it with the service target, then check whether the encoder cable clamp is pulling the body upward or rearward against its bracket. |
| T4 | Run an orientation cycle and a low-speed spindle hold with the coupling still visible, then compare the feedback behavior before and after lightly seating the coupling by hand. If orientation repeatability improves when the rear hub is pushed back against the shaft shoulder, keep the fault on the coupling stack and not on the encoder electronics. |
| T5 | Measure clamp-screw breakout torque and inspect the motor-side hub bore, shaft flat, and bracket alignment. A gap that is too large, breakout torque below the marked value, or a cracked insert that has shed dust into the lower cover points to a mechanical coupling boundary rather than to feedback noise from the control cabinet. |
| T6 | Restore hub position, set the coupling gap to the specified range, replace the insert if damaged, and torque both clamp screws while the encoder body is held square to the bracket. Confirm that the cable clamp holds the harness without lifting the encoder and that both hubs remain seated against their intended shaft positions. |
| T7 | Release the spindle only after repeated orientation cycles and a repeated speed hold show stable feedback with no renewed hub movement, no insert dust, and no clamp-mark shift. The accepted boundary is a corrected encoder-coupling assembly, not a vague cleared alarm, and the note must state which hub, insert, or bracket condition was actually restored. |
