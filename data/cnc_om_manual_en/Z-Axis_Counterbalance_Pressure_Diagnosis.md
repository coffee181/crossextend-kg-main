| T1 | On Aurex VMC-740, record the Z-axis complaint as head drift, uneven upward motion, or counterbalance pressure alarm, and note the spindle mass condition and Z height where it appears. Capture the as-received regulator pressure, any observed vertical creep, and the exact axis position before the counterbalance circuit is opened. |
| T2 | Secure the vertical axis mechanically and expose regulator PR1, gauge PG1, the check valve, the hose from regulator to cylinder, the upper cylinder fitting, and the rod-side connection. Keep the upper pneumatic path and the lower moving axis state visible together so pressure loss can be compared with actual Z drift. |
| T3 | Measure regulator pressure at rest and during a short commanded Z stroke, then compare both readings with the service target for the installed spindle mass. If pressure is correct at the regulator but falls at the cylinder fitting during motion, keep the boundary on hose, check valve, or cylinder-side leakage rather than on the gauge setting alone. |
| T4 | Inspect PR1 adjustment lock, PG1 response, hose condition, fitting threads, and the cylinder connection from upper regulator manifold to lower rod-side fitting. A damaged gauge can imitate a pressure problem, while a wet fitting or hissing hose points to a real leak path even before the head begins to creep. |
| T5 | Hold the head at the complaint position with the machine in a safe monitored state and measure actual creep over time while reading gauge stability. Stable pressure with measurable downward drift pushes the boundary toward cylinder sealing or brake interaction, whereas dropping pressure with corresponding drift keeps the fault on the pneumatic side. |
| T6 | Correct only the identified pressure or connection issue, then restore regulator lock, hose routing, and fitting support so the line is not strained by Z travel. Confirm that the hose enters the cylinder fitting without side load and that the gauge and regulator remain mounted squarely on the manifold plate. |
| T7 | Repeat the short Z stroke and the stationary hold at the original complaint height, comparing pressure behavior and measured drift with the pre-repair condition. The axis cannot be released on one stable gauge reading if head position still changes once the counterbalance circuit sees real stroke demand. |
| T8 | Close the work only after the report names the exact counterbalance boundary, such as regulator setting, faulty gauge PG1, check-valve leakage, hose leak, or cylinder-side loss, and ties it to measured pressure and drift. Accept the repair when Z motion is smooth and the head remains stable at the original hold position with the corrected counterbalance path restored. |
