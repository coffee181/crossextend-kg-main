| T1 | On Aurex VMC-760, record the Y-axis complaint as dimensional drift on reversal, knocking near center travel, or following error that appears after warm-up, and note the exact Y position where backlash is visible. Capture the measured lost motion, table load, and thermal state before any hardware is loosened. |
| T2 | Expose Y-axis ball screw, fixed-end support BY-FE1, floating-end support BY-FL1, motor coupling CP-Y1, ball-nut housing, and the front pair of guide trucks while preserving paint witness and lubrication marks. Keep screw support and nut housing visible together so lost motion can be assigned to one mechanical boundary instead of to the axis generally. |
| T3 | Measure backlash at the table with a dial indicator while commanding controlled reversals, then compare table motion with screw rotation and coupling rotation. If the screw turns before the table responds while the coupling stays synchronized with the motor, the next inspection should stay on the nut housing or support stack rather than on the servo tune. |
| T4 | Inspect ball-nut mounting screws, support-bearing locknut on BY-FE1, coupling clamp bolts on CP-Y1, and guide-truck seats for witness movement or fretting. A loose nut mount creates different evidence than fixed-end preload loss, so upper nut-housing marks and lower support-housing marks should be read separately instead of being merged into one backlash label. |
| T5 | Compare backlash and mechanical response at the complaint position with a second Y-axis position outside the worn zone. If lost motion grows in one travel band while support witness remains stable, the boundary moves toward nut housing, screw wear, or local mount looseness rather than toward uniform support-bearing preload loss. |
| T6 | Tighten or repair only the identified boundary, then remeasure backlash with the same indicator point and the same reversal command. Confirm that the corrected fasteners or support parts are seated against their lands and that the coupling and guide-truck hardware remain in their original supported geometry after the adjustment. |
| T7 | Run the axis through the original warm reversal window and compare backlash, following error, and audible knock with the as-found condition. The release decision must come from the same motion band that created the complaint and not from a short cold jog near one end of travel. |
| T8 | Close the work only after the report names the exact backlash boundary, such as ball-nut mounting screws, fixed-end preload stack BY-FE1, coupling clamp slip at CP-Y1, or localized screw wear, and ties it to the measured change in lost motion. Accept the repair when repeated Y-axis reversals remain quiet and dimensionally stable through the original warm travel zone. |
