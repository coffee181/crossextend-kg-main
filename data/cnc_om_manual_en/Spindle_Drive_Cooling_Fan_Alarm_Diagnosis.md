| T1 | On Helion DriveCab-610, record the drive-cooling complaint as fan alarm from FAN-DRV1, absent airflow, or thermal rise at the spindle drive, and note cabinet ambient temperature and drive heat-sink temperature before removal. Capture whether the fault appears at power-up or only after sustained spindle duty. |
| T2 | Expose cooling fan FAN-DRV1, fan connector XF-31, finger guard, intake filter, heat-sink fins, and the local airflow channel without disturbing the dust pattern. Keep the fan and the heat sink visible together so a failed motor can be separated from a blocked airflow path. |
| T3 | Measure fan supply voltage, fan current, and heat-sink temperature during the cooling command, then compare those values with airflow at the outlet side of the cabinet. If the fan receives proper voltage but airflow remains low with a dust-packed filter, keep the boundary on filter or airflow obstruction rather than on the fan motor alone. |
| T4 | Inspect connector retention, blade condition, filter media, and fin blockage from upper intake to lower exhaust path. A broken blade, a loose connector, and a clogged heat sink all create thermal alarms, but they show different evidence in current draw, noise, and airflow direction. |
| T5 | Replace or clean only the failed fan-side hardware, then restore the connector and airflow channel with the guard and filter in their production positions. Confirm that the fan rotates in the correct direction and that the harness and guard do not touch the blade arc. |
| T6 | Run the drive through the original thermal load and compare fan current, heat-sink temperature trend, and alarm behavior with the as-found condition. The cabinet is not released on one spinning fan if the thermal rise still follows the same path under real spindle duty. |
| T7 | Reinspect the guard screws, connector latch, filter seating, and cable support after the live run because some fan repairs pass cold and fail once the cabinet warms. This second inspection confirms that airflow and support geometry remain stable after heat returns. |
| T8 | Close the work only after the report names the exact failed boundary, such as fan motor FAN-DRV1, connector XF-31, blocked filter, or heat-sink obstruction, and ties it to measured voltage, current, airflow, and temperature trend. Accept the repair when the drive remains thermally stable with no renewed fan alarm under the original load condition. |
