| T1 | On Velorian VMC-900, record the through-spindle coolant complaint as leakage at pressure only, leakage only with spindle rotation, or drip after shutoff, and note whether coolant first appears at the spindle nose, rotary union RU-1 vent, or rear housing. Capture the pressure setpoint and the spindle speed window that reproduces the leak. |
| T2 | Expose rotary union RU-1, inlet fitting JIC-08, vent line, drain line, spindle rear interface, and pressure gauge PG-TSC while preserving the original residue field on the union body. Keep the union housing and the adjacent spindle interface visible together so tracking coolant is not mistaken for the true leak origin. |
| T3 | Pressurize the TSC circuit with the spindle stopped and inspect the inlet fitting, vent port, housing joint, and spindle interface for the first fresh wet point. If no leak appears at standstill, keep the pressure state and union dry field documented before moving to the rotation test. |
| T4 | Run the spindle through the original speed window with TSC active and compare leakage pattern, vent behavior, and drain return with the static test. A leak that appears only under rotation belongs to seal-face behavior or a rotating interface, whereas a leak that starts static belongs to fittings, housing joints, or the stationary spindle interface. |
| T5 | Inspect the vent line and drain path so normal seal drainage is separated from an actual housing or seal leak, then compare inlet-fitting wetting with union-body wetting. This split prevents a correct drain path from being misread as a failed rotary seal or a loose inlet fitting from being blamed on the union cartridge. |
| T6 | Correct only the identified fitting, drain, vent, or union boundary and restore hose routing so the vent and drain lines fall continuously toward their intended path. Confirm that inlet fitting JIC-08 seats squarely, the vent line is not kinked, and the drain is not trapped above the union body. |
| T7 | Repeat both the static pressure test and the rotating test through the original speed window while watching the corrected boundary from inlet fitting to vent to spindle interface. The spindle cannot be released on one dry static pass if the seal leaks again once rotation and coolant pressure are combined. |
| T8 | Close the work only after the report names the exact leaking boundary, such as inlet fitting, rotary-seal vent path, housing joint, drain issue, or spindle interface, and ties it to the specific operating condition that reproduced it. Accept the repair when the TSC branch stays dry at the corrected point through both static pressure and the original rotating condition. |
