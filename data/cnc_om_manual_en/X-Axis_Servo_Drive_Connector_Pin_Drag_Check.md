| T1 | On Novaris DriveCab-780, record the X-axis drive complaint as intermittent trip, missing enable, or encoder fault, and identify the connector family, for example M23 at XDR-X1, and the axis motion window that reproduces the issue. Capture the alarm code and whether the fault appears only when the harness flexes near the drive. |
| T2 | Isolate cabinet power and expose drive-side motor or feedback connector XDR-X1, latch, front cavity face, rear seal, cable clamp, and the first supported harness bend while preserving the as-found strain. Keep the connector body and the first support point visible together so terminal condition can be compared with cable-induced load. |
| T3 | Inspect the suspect cavities for recessed pins, weak drag, heat tint, spread terminals, rear-seal pullback, or broken latch features, then compare them with healthy adjacent cavities on the same connector. A weak terminal and a strained rear seal can create the same intermittent trip, but they belong to different repair boundaries. |
| T4 | Use the approved drag tool or mating pin to measure terminal retention and compare the value at the suspect cavity with a healthy cavity. If terminal drag is weak only at one position while the connector shell and cable clamp remain stable, keep the boundary on that terminal cavity rather than on the whole harness branch. |
| T5 | Re-seat or replace only the affected terminal, seal, or connector hardware, then restore the harness through the original clamp so the first bend begins after the rear seal. Confirm that the latch locks fully, the rear seal sits evenly, and the cable clamp holds the harness without twisting the connector body upward. |
| T6 | Close the work only after repeated axis enable and motion through the original fault window leave the corrected cavity stable and the report names the exact connector family, cavity position, and measured terminal condition. Accept the repair when the X axis no longer trips under the same harness-loading condition at connector XDR-X1 that originally exposed the fault. |
