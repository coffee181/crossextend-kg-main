| T1 | On Arcline MillCenter-700, record the chip-conveyor complaint as chain jump, excessive slack, or uneven movement, and note chip type, conveyor load, and whether the problem appears at startup or after sustained run. Capture the as-found sag at take-up station TU-1 and the exact location of the jump or noise before the hardware is moved. |
| T2 | Lock out the conveyor and expose drive sprocket DS-1, tail sprocket TS-1, chain flights, take-up bolts, side rails, return channel, and gearbox output while preserving the chip distribution. Keep the drive end and tail end visible together so slack, misalignment, and jam points can be compared across the full chain path. |
| T3 | Measure chain sag and compare left and right chain alignment at the service reference points, then inspect whether one strand has climbed the sprocket tooth or whether compacted chips are forcing the return section upward. If one side carries more sag or one take-up bolt sits farther out than the other, keep the boundary on uneven adjustment or side loading instead of on general chain wear. |
| T4 | Inspect take-up threads, tail-shaft bearings, sprocket teeth, chain pins, and side-rail contact from upper loaded run to lower return run. A worn sprocket and a loose take-up create different evidence at the tooth root, chain pitch, and tail-shaft alignment, so those points should be read separately before correction. |
| T5 | Adjust take-up evenly, clear trapped chips, and confirm that chain engagement remains centered on both sprockets without pulling one side into the rail. The corrected chain should run with balanced left-right sag and no side-rail rub when the conveyor is turned by hand through the original problem zone. |
| T6 | Run the conveyor under controlled load and observe chain tracking, gearbox sound, and flight movement through a full cycle from head to tail. The machine cannot be released on a quiet empty run if the chain jumps again once chips enter the loaded section near TS-1 return entry. |
| T7 | Reinspect take-up bolts, tail bearings, chain pitch line, and side-rail clearance after the powered run, then close the work only after the report names the exact failed boundary, such as uneven take-up, worn drive sprocket, tail-bearing play, side-rail interference, or chip jam in the return channel, and ties it to final chain tracking. Accept the repair when the conveyor moves smoothly with balanced chain tension and no renewed jump through the original load condition. |
