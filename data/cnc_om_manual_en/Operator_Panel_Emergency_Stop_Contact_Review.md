| T1 | On Velorian ControlPanel-500, record the emergency-stop complaint as no reset, intermittent trip, or high contact resistance, and identify the exact panel location of the affected button. Capture the safety-chain state and any related alarm before the panel hardware is disturbed. |
| T2 | Isolate control power as required and expose E-stop button body ES-1, contact block CB-ES1, mounting nut, terminal pair on XT-SAFE, and the first harness support while preserving wire order and the as-found button position. Keep the actuator and the contact block visible together so mechanical push travel can be compared with electrical change. |
| T3 | Measure contact state and, if appropriate, loaded voltage drop through the press and release cycle instead of relying on button feel alone. If the mechanical latch resets normally but electrical resistance remains high, keep the boundary on contact block CB-ES1 or terminal pair rather than on the red mushroom head. |
| T4 | Inspect terminal retention, contact-block seating, actuator stem travel, mounting-nut torque, and panel cutout wear from front button flange to rear contact face. A loose mounting nut, worn block latch, or oxidized terminal can all trip the chain, but they show different evidence in movement, drag, and measured electrical behavior. |
| T5 | Replace or restore only the failed button, contact block, or terminal hardware, then reassemble the stack in the correct order so the actuator fully engages the contact block. Confirm that the wiring exits with no side load on the contact block and that the panel opening does not allow the body to rotate under use. |
| T6 | Repeat press and reset cycles and compare contact state or loaded-drop values with the pre-repair condition while the full safety chain remains active. The panel cannot be released on one clean reset if the same contact pair still opens intermittently after repeated use. |
| T7 | Reinspect mounting nut, contact-block latch, terminal screws, and harness support after the repeated cycles because control-panel faults often return once the button settles under real use. This second inspection confirms that the corrected safety path remains mechanically stable. |
| T8 | Close the work only after the report names the exact failed boundary, such as E-stop contact block CB-ES1, button body ES-1, mounting nut, or terminal pair on XT-SAFE, and ties it to measured chain response. Accept the repair when the safety chain opens and resets repeatably with no renewed intermittent trip or excessive contact resistance. |
