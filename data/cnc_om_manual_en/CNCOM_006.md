| Time step | O&M sample text |
|---|---|
| T1 | On Kestrel VMC-700, record the complaint as no tool unclamp, delayed release during tool change, or coil alarm on the unclamp circuit, and note both the machine air or hydraulic supply state and the last successful tool release. The work order should already name solenoid valve SV3 and the drawbar release path as the suspected boundary. |
| T2 | Expose solenoid valve SV3, its 24 V coil, manual override, supply regulator, upstream gauge port, downstream release line, M12 connector, and the drawbar release cylinder path. Keep the electrical side and the pressure side visible together because the same no-unclamp complaint can be created by a dead coil or by a live coil driving a stuck spool. |
| T3 | Measure coil voltage during the unclamp command and compare coil resistance with the service value before the valve body is disturbed. Record whether the connector latch is fully seated and whether the cable is routed through the bracket clip, because a loose connector can imitate an intermittent coil failure while leaving the valve body healthy. |
| T4 | Measure upstream and downstream pressure during the same unclamp request, then compare pressure recovery with the coil command timing. If upstream pressure reaches the target while downstream pressure barely rises, keep the next inspection on the spool, exhaust path, and valve body instead of chasing the supply regulator or the drawbar hardware first. |
| T5 | Remove the cartridge or valve body as the service method allows and inspect the spool, bore, O-rings, exhaust silencer, and contamination caught at the lower outlet. A varnished spool or blocked exhaust can hold the same pressure signature as a weak coil, so the electrical and pneumatic evidence must be read together before the boundary is assigned. |
| T6 | Isolate the fault only after the coil, connector, and pressure measurements are compared with the spool condition. If electrical command is correct and the downstream line recovers immediately after the cleaned or replaced valve is installed, keep the fault on SV3 spool contamination or valve-body wear rather than on the release cylinder or the upstream regulator. |
| T7 | Restore the valve, reconnect the M12 plug, and verify that downstream release pressure and drawbar stroke now follow the unclamp command through repeated tool-change requests. Confirm that the release line remains dry, the connector is held by its clip, and the manual override returns fully after each cycle. |
| T8 | Close the work only after the note states the exact failed item, such as SV3 coil, connector terminal, spool contamination, exhaust blockage, or valve-body wear, and ties it to both electrical and pressure measurements. Accept the repair when actual tool release is repeated with the same holder and the corrected solenoid path remains stable under normal supply pressure. |
