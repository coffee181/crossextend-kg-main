| T1 | On Helion VMC-620, record the complaint as Y-axis thrust-bearing temperature rise during long feed, high rapid traverse, or repeated reversals, and note the speed band and lubrication state that reproduce it. Capture the first warm reading and the travel zone in which the support housing becomes noticeably hotter than the surrounding casting. |
| T2 | Expose Y-axis fixed-end thrust-bearing housing BY-FE2, locknut, grease fitting GR-Y1, seal carrier, coupling-side shaft extension, and adjacent support block without disturbing grease witness or temperature tape. Keep upper housing cap and lower support face visible together so heat source and support seating can be judged in one view. |
| T3 | Run the Y-axis through the complaint cycle and measure housing temperature at BY-FE2, screw temperature at TS-YS1, and ambient reference temperature at the same time. Compare those values with the opposite end or with a known good machine value so the diagnosis does not rely on touch or operator impression alone. |
| T4 | Inspect locknut witness, grease condition, seal lip drag, and housing seating marks immediately after the hot run while the support remains expanded. Excess preload, dry grease, and seal drag can all raise temperature, but they leave different evidence on the upper locknut, lower seal edge, and grease purge path. |
| T5 | Measure rotational drag or bearing breakaway as permitted by the service method, then compare the result with the hot temperature pattern. If drag is high and grease is healthy, keep the boundary on preload or seal compression; if drag is normal but grease is dark or absent, keep the boundary on lubrication quality or grease path instead. |
| T6 | Loosen only what is required to correct the identified preload or seal issue, then restore locknut position, grease condition, and support seating against the mounting land. Confirm that the shaft extension remains centered through the seal and that the housing cap does not rock on the support face after retightening. |
| T7 | Repeat the same feed and reversal cycle, then compare hot housing temperature and noise with the original readings. The machine cannot be released on a cooler first minute if the support still climbs to the same abnormal temperature by the end of the full motion cycle. |
| T8 | Inspect the support again after the second hot run for renewed witness movement, grease purge, or seal drag that appeared only after thermal expansion. This follow-up check keeps the release boundary on real bearing behavior and not on a temporary cold adjustment. |
| T9 | Close the work only after the report names the exact hot-running boundary, such as locknut over-preload, grease deficit at GR-Y1, seal drag, or internal thrust-bearing wear in BY-FE2, and ties it to measured temperature and drag. Accept the repair when the Y-axis support remains thermally stable through the original motion cycle with no renewed noise or preload shift. |
