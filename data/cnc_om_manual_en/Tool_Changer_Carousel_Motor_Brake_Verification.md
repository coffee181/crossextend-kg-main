| T1 | On Kestrel ToolChange-555, record the carousel complaint as brake overrun after station stop, failure to hold the indexed pocket, or drift when power is removed, and note the station and direction in which the fault is repeatable. Capture the current stop error and any alarm history before the brake housing is opened. |
| T2 | Expose the carousel motor, brake housing, brake connector, gearbox input hub, station timing marks, and the stop-position sensor while preserving the motor-to-gearbox relationship and witness paint. Keep the electrical brake path and the mechanical index path visible together so stop drift can be assigned to one side of the system. |
| T3 | Measure brake command voltage and current during index and at stop, then compare the timing of brake energize and de-energize with actual carousel motion. If the command is correct but the wheel continues past the same stop mark, keep the boundary on the brake pack or gearbox backlash rather than on the control output alone. |
| T4 | Inspect brake-coil resistance, connector retention, gearbox input witness, and the station timing marks while the brake remains assembled. A slipping gearbox and a weak brake can both create stop drift, but witness movement on the input hub points to mechanical slip while stable witness and poor electrical values point to the brake circuit. |
| T5 | Compare hold behavior with power on and power off states if the service method allows it, then inspect whether the carousel remains within the station tolerance after the stop. This split between energized and de-energized hold behavior keeps the diagnosis on brake release or hold function rather than on index timing generally. |
| T6 | Remove or repair only the failed brake or input-hub hardware, restore the connector and timing relationship, and verify that the motor and gearbox remain seated in their original alignment. Confirm that the connector lock is engaged, the brake housing fasteners are seated, and the timing marks return to their prescribed relationship at station stop. |
| T7 | Run repeated index cycles in both directions through several stations while measuring stop repeatability and watching whether the brake engages at the same point each time. The release boundary is not one accurate stop but repeated stable stops through the same station range that originally drifted. |
| T8 | Reinspect brake housing, connector, hub witness, and station sensor after the repeated cycles because some carousel brakes pass the first cycle and begin to drift again once heat builds in the motor stack. This second inspection confirms that the corrected brake or hub remains stable after real use. |
| T9 | Record stop error before and after repair, brake electrical values, and the final station-repeatability result in one continuous closeout note. The report must make clear whether the recovered boundary was electrical, mechanical, or both. |
| T10 | Close the work only after the note names the exact failed boundary, such as brake coil, brake connector, friction pack, or gearbox input-hub slip, and ties it to measured stop behavior across repeated stations. Accept the repair when the carousel stops and holds on station with no renewed overrun in the tested forward and reverse cycles. |
