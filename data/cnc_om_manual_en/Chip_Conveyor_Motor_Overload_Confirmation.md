| T1 | On Helion MillCenter-560, record the conveyor overload complaint from relay OL-CV1, whether it trips at startup or after continuous run, and the chip type present in the conveyor when the overload occurs. Note the overload setting, motor nameplate current, and whether the machine has already shown conveyor drag or jam noise. |
| T2 | Expose the conveyor motor M-CV1, gearbox GB-CV1, overload relay OL-CV1, drive chain or sprocket, head shaft, tail roller, and loaded chip section without clearing all chips first. Keep the electrical path and the mechanical drive path visible together so overload cause can be placed on one side of the system instead of guessed from the alarm alone. |
| T3 | Measure motor current at startup and during loaded run, then compare those values with mechanical free movement of the conveyor with power isolated. If current spikes while the chain is hard to turn by hand, keep the next split on mechanical drag, chip jam, or bearing seizure rather than on the overload relay setting. |
| T4 | Inspect drive chain tension, gearbox output, head-shaft bearings, tail roller, and chip packing in the trough from upper drive to lower return. A seized bearing, overpacked trough, and overloaded motor produce different evidence in heat, drag location, and current rise, so those clues need separate lines in the report. |
| T5 | Inspect the overload relay setting, contactor condition, motor cooling path, and terminal-box connections only after the mechanical branch has been checked. This keeps a true electrical problem from being hidden by coincidental chip load and prevents a mechanical jam from being blamed on a correctly responding overload relay. |
| T6 | Correct the identified mechanical or electrical boundary, restore chain path and wiring support, and then rerun the conveyor under the same chip load that originally caused the trip. Confirm that the motor current and the overload relay behavior now follow the corrected boundary and not a temporary unloaded condition. |
| T7 | Reinspect terminal box, overload relay, drive chain, and the loaded trough after the first powered run because many overload faults reappear once heat returns to the motor or chips settle back into the same trough geometry. This second inspection proves the recovery holds under real operating conditions. |
| T8 | Close the work only after the report names the exact overload source, such as chip jam, seized tail roller, drive-chain drag, gearbox drag, motor fault, or overload-relay setting, and ties it to measured current and rerun behavior. Accept the repair when the conveyor completes the original loaded run without renewed OL-CV1 trip and with stable motor and drive conditions. |
