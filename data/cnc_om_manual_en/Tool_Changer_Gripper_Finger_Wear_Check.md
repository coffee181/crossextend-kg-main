| Time step | O&M sample text |
|---|---|
| T1 | On Velorian ToolChange-592, record the complaint as weak gripper hold, transfer slip, or visible finger wear on the right arm side, and identify the specific finger pair before disassembly. Note the toolholder type and whether the slip occurs during pickup, transfer, or pocket return. |
| T2 | Expose the right-side gripper fingers, pivot pins, torsion springs, finger pads, and the local arm-side stop while holding the toolchanger arm in a safe service position. Keep both fingers and the tool-flange contact path visible together so asymmetrical wear can be assigned to one side of the gripper assembly. |
| T3 | Inspect finger tips, spring preload, pivot-pin wear, and contact witness on the tool flange from upper finger face to lower pad edge. Compare left and right finger geometry because one relaxed spring or one worn finger pad can tilt the holder while the opposite side still appears serviceable. |
| T4 | Install a verified holder and measure centering and grip at the right gripper pair, then compare with the opposite gripper or with service reference values. If the holder sits off center with normal pivot freedom, keep the boundary on finger wear or spring load instead of on the arm stop or pocket position. |
| T5 | Replace or adjust only the failed finger components, restore spring seating and pivot retention, and confirm that both fingers close symmetrically against the holder flange. The repaired pair must center the holder with equal pad contact and no side bias from the arm stop or nearby bracket. |
| T6 | Run dry transfers through the original pickup and return path and inspect the repaired fingers again for pad movement, spring seating, or renewed asymmetrical contact. The changer is not released on one successful grip if the finger pair loses centering after the second or third transfer. |
| T7 | Close the work only after the report names the exact failed boundary, such as right lower finger pad, torsion spring loss, pivot-pin wear, or arm-stop bias, and ties it to final grip and centering behavior. Accept the repair when the verified holder transfers repeatedly with stable gripper alignment and no renewed slip on the corrected side. |
