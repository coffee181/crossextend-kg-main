| T1 | On Kestrel ShopAir-910, begin the utility-pressure recovery by naming the pneumatic or hydraulic maintenance completed and the machine functions that depend on restored supply, such as chuck clamp, air blast, door cylinder, or tool clamp. Record inlet pressure at PG-IN1, regulator setpoint, and the original failed functions before pressure is returned. |
| T2 | Restore utility pressure gradually and observe gauge response at main inlet PG-IN1, FRL outlet PG-OUT1, and at least one downstream functional branch rather than trusting a single cabinet gauge. Keep the main regulator, FRL bowl, and one representative consumer path visible together so the recovery can be tied to a real supply chain. |
| T3 | Check for inlet decay, regulator overshoot, isolation valves left closed, and drain leakage before commanding any machine function. If pressure is correct at the inlet but not at the consumer branch, keep the boundary on the intervening regulator, FRL, or shutoff hardware instead of on the consumer itself. |
| T4 | Exercise representative consumers one at a time, such as chuck clamp, door cylinder, and air blast, and record pressure response and mechanical result at each branch. This split between supply recovery and branch function prevents a good regulator reading from hiding one downstream branch that still cannot deliver real pressure under flow. |
| T5 | Reinspect serviced fittings, drains, regulator locks, hose supports, and cable supports after the first function group because minor leaks and unset regulators often appear only once the machine returns to real demand. Compare the as-run pressure path with the as-restored state captured before the proof. |
| T6 | Run a second proof group with the machine in its normal stopped and then active condition, confirming that pressure holds during standby and recovers correctly after demand. The utility system is not released on one brief actuation if pressure drifts away again once the branches stop cycling. |
| T7 | Record inlet pressure at PG-IN1, regulated pressure at PG-OUT1, downstream consumer response, and any final fitting or regulator corrections in one traceable note. The closeout must show how utility pressure was proven from main supply to the named functional branches. |
| T8 | Close the work only after the report names the restored utility boundary, such as main regulator, FRL, shutoff path, or one downstream branch, and ties it to repeated stable function under demand and standby. Accept the recovery when the original affected functions operate normally and the utility pressure path remains stable in the fully restored machine state. |
