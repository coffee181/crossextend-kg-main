| T1 | On Velorian VMC-690, record where rust was found on the Z-axis guide rail and whether it followed coolant overflow, long idle storage, or poor way-oil coverage. Capture the affected rail span, the truck travel range over that span, and the as-found oil film before any cleaning begins. |
| T2 | Expose the affected guide-rail section, the nearest guide truck, the wiper path, the lubrication point, and the cover edge while preserving the rust boundary and the dry or wet transition. Keep the upper cover exit and the lower rail span visible together so the source path can be read from leak or bypass to corrosion zone. |
| T3 | Measure rust length and maximum pit or stain depth, then compare the damaged span with a clean reference span farther down the rail. If corrosion starts exactly where the wiper stops sealing or where the lube film disappears, keep the boundary on wiper bypass or oil delivery instead of treating the rust as random environmental damage. |
| T4 | Inspect the wiper lip, lube point, drain path, and cover edge for the hardware condition that allowed moisture to remain on the rail. A failed wiper leaves a different pattern than a blocked lube point, so upper wipe marks and lower oil film should be recorded separately before the rail is treated. |
| T5 | Clean and protect the rail only within the allowed service limits, then confirm whether the affected zone is surface film or true pitting that threatens guide accuracy. Compare the post-clean surface with the measured pit depth so the report makes clear whether the rail remains serviceable or requires escalation. |
| T6 | Run the Z-axis through the affected zone and inspect whether the guide truck and wiper now pass the cleaned span with restored oil coverage and no fresh moisture retention. The machine cannot be released if the same span dries out or rewets immediately under the normal travel path. |
| T7 | Close the work only after the report names the exact rail span, rust depth, likely cause such as wiper bypass or lube deficit, and the final acceptance status of the rail. Accept the review when the cleaned span remains protected through Z travel and the moisture or oil path that created the corrosion has been clearly assigned. |
