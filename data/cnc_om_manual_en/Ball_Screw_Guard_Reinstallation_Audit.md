| T1 | When reinstalling the X-axis rear way-cover assembly on Velorian MillCenter-650, record whether the audit is driven by cover drag, missing fasteners, slat noise, or proximity to the ballscrew and guide trucks after service. Identify the exact left, center, and right cover sections removed so the closeout follows the real hardware stack instead of a generic cover check. |
| T2 | Offer the rear cover assembly into place with side rails, upper and lower wipers, rear support brackets BR-R1 and BR-R2, and ballscrew tube all visible before any fastener is tightened fully. Keep the rear cover lip and nearest guide-truck caps in the same inspection window because cover interference usually shows up at the rail-to-wiper path first. |
| T3 | Start mounting screws loosely and measure cover-to-ballscrew clearance, overlap between adjacent slats, and wiper seating at left and right ends of travel. A cover that clears the screw tube at center travel but closes to less than 2 mm near right rear bracket BR-R2 should be treated as a bracket or rail issue, not as acceptable fit. |
| T4 | Inspect the side-rail straightness, slat overlap, wiper orientation, rear bracket shims, lube-line support, and the nearby sensor-cable clip from the upper rail down to the lower fixed bracket. Rub marks on one slat edge, twisted wiper lips, or a lube tube pushed into the slat path all point to different reassembly boundaries. |
| T5 | Correct only the failed rail, bracket, shim stack, wiper, or support clip, then retighten the assembly in sequence from the fixed center section outward so the cover settles naturally across the travel path. Confirm that the side rails stay parallel and that the slats compress and extend without pulling the lower wiper off the guide surface. |
| T6 | Traverse the axis through the original feed band and reversal points with the way cover fully installed, then compare cover noise, drag, and final clearances with the as-found condition. The machine should not be cleared on a static fit check if the same rear travel still produces cover knock, wiper roll, or ballscrew contact. |
| T7 | Close this reinstallation only after the note names the exact cover section or support boundary corrected and records the final left-to-right travel result with the machine in guarded condition. The accepted state is a rear cover that moves quietly through full stroke, keeps safe clearance to the ballscrew and guide trucks, and leaves wipers W-RU and W-RL seated evenly at both ends. |
