| T1 | On Aurex VMC-750, record the coolant complaint as weak flood stream at the spindle ring, startup cavitation at pump P-1, or low-pressure alarm from switch PS-C1, and note tank level, refractometer reading, and the exact operating mode where flow collapses. Capture whether the issue appears only in flood mode or also when the through-spindle branch is isolated. |
| T2 | Expose the centrifugal pump, suction hose, suction strainer cap, discharge hose, check valve, motor starter leads, and the branch to the flood manifold while preserving the as-found hose shape. Keep the upstream tank-to-pump path and the downstream pump-to-manifold path visible together so loss of prime can be separated from discharge restriction. |
| T3 | Measure three-phase supply voltage and motor current during pump start, then compare suction behavior and discharge pressure at gauge PG-C2 under the same command. If the motor starts normally but current drops while the suction hose shows air pockets and discharge pressure never rises to the required band, keep the boundary on prime loss or suction restriction rather than on the motor itself. |
| T4 | Inspect the suction strainer mesh, hose wall, lower pickup foot, gasket at the strainer cap, and the suction-clamp sealing line from tank outlet to pump inlet. A clogged strainer, split suction hose, or leaking cap gasket can all produce the same weak stream, but they leave different evidence in air ingress, debris load, and clamp witness. |
| T5 | Isolate the branch by priming pump P-1 manually and remeasuring discharge pressure with flood manifold MV-F1 open and then closed. If pressure recovers only after the suction side is sealed or cleared while the discharge branch remains unchanged, keep the fault on suction restriction or air ingress instead of replacing the pump or the nozzle manifold. |
| T6 | Correct the failed suction-side hardware, restore the hose through its original clips, and confirm that the hose bend radius and strainer-cap seal are stable from upper tank wall to lower pump inlet. The repaired branch should sit against its clips without flattening the hose or pulling the cap out of square. |
| T7 | Run the pump through repeated starts and through the original flood-demand condition while watching discharge pressure, nozzle stream, and cavitation noise. The system cannot be released on one primed start if the pump loses prime again after the first off cycle or once the machine vibrates at normal duty. |
| T8 | Close the work only after the report names the exact limiting boundary, such as clogged strainer mesh, collapsed suction hose, strainer-cap gasket leak, pickup obstruction, or failed pump motor, and ties it to measured voltage, current, and discharge pressure. Accept the repair when the flood branch delivers stable flow with no renewed cavitation under the same demand that originally produced the complaint. |
