|---|---|
| T1 | On Helion ToolChange-580, record the changer-arm complaint as clamp-cylinder leakage, weak grip on the tool flange, or delayed arm close during transfer, and note supply pressure at the manifold before any line is opened. Capture whether the fault appears on the left gripper, right gripper, or both sides of the double-arm assembly. |
| T2 | Expose the double-arm clamp cylinder, rod end, head end cap, supply hose, return or exhaust port, rod clevis, and the gripper linkage while preserving the as-found rod extension. Keep the cylinder body and the gripper link visible together so fluid loss and mechanical side load can be read as one chain instead of two separate symptoms. |
| T3 | Inspect the rod seal, head-cap O-ring, hose fittings, and clevis alignment for oil or air trace from upstream supply to downstream gripper link. A wet rod with a clean cap seam belongs to rod-seal wear, while a dry rod and bubbling at the port fitting pushes the boundary toward the fitting or manifold face instead. |
| T4 | Command dry clamp and unclamp cycles and record rod stroke, response time, and pressure drop across the cylinder during each event. If stroke is short and pressure collapses when the rod reaches the same end of travel, keep the boundary on seal leakage or cap bypass rather than on the control valve or arm timing cam. |
| T5 | Compare the suspect cylinder behavior with the opposite gripper side and inspect the mounting bracket and clevis for side load that could cut the seal prematurely. Uneven clevis angle or bracket twist that pushes the rod sideways belongs to the support path, even if the first visible symptom is oil at the rod seal. |
| T6 | Replace or reseal the failed cylinder components, restore the hose and clevis alignment, and confirm that the rod enters and exits the cylinder squarely with no bracket preload. The hose should be routed through its clip without twisting the end cap, and the clevis pin should sit centered in the gripper link. |
| T7 | Repeat clamp and unclamp cycles through several arm positions while watching rod travel, gripper force, and pressure stability on both sides of the changer arm. The release boundary is not one dry static cylinder but stable gripping behavior through the real arm travel envelope. |
| T8 | Reinspect the rod, cap seam, fittings, and bracket after the repeated cycles because many toolchanger cylinder jobs pass the first stroke and start leaking again once the arm settles into its final path. This second inspection confirms the corrected seal and support geometry remain stable. |
| T9 | Close the work only after the report names the exact failed boundary, such as rod seal, head-cap seal, hose fitting, or clevis side load, and ties it to measured pressure, stroke, and grip behavior. Accept the repair when the changer arm clamps the test holder repeatedly with no renewed leak and no delayed cylinder response. |
