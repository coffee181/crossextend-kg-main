| Time step | O&M sample text |
|---|---|
| T1 | On Kestrel VMC-720, record the X-axis complaint as growl or rumble from the fixed-end support between 6 m/min and 12 m/min, and note whether the sound grows after ten minutes of jogging or changes with reversal. Capture housing temperature, axis load, and the exact travel window near the left support before any cover is removed. |
| T2 | Expose X-axis fixed-end support housing BX-FE1, locknut, duplex bearing pair, lip seal, coupling-side shaft extension, and lower way-cover end plate while leaving lubrication film and witness paint intact. Keep left support and nearby coupling end visible together so transmitted noise is not confused with a true bearing source. |
| T3 | Traverse the axis through the complaint window and compare vibration at support housing BX-FE1 with vibration at nut housing and at motor bracket. If support housing shows the highest vibration while nut housing remains comparatively quiet, keep the fault path on the fixed end rather than on the traveling saddle or coupling cover. |
| T4 | Measure support-housing temperature and axial float at the screw end, then compare those values with the accepted machine target or with the opposite end if the layout allows. Elevated temperature with measurable float usually points to preload loss or a loosened locknut, whereas normal temperature with noise can push the boundary toward seal drag or cover interference. |
| T5 | Inspect the locknut witness, bearing retainer screws, lip-seal contact, and grease purge condition from upper cap to lower drain edge. A shifted locknut witness, dry purge, or metal dust at the lower seal lip is stronger evidence of bearing or preload failure than general noise reported by the operator. |
| T6 | Isolate the boundary only after vibration, temperature, and float are compared with the as-found hardware state. If vibration drops when end float is removed manually and the locknut witness has moved, keep the fault on the fixed-end bearing preload stack instead of blaming the ball nut, servo tuning, or the motor coupling. |
| T7 | Restore preload, secure the locknut, and recheck the housing with the way-cover end plate and lubrication lines returned to their original positions. Confirm that the support block is seated against the machine base, the seal lip is not rolled, and the coupling-side shaft extension is not pulled sideways by the cover or bracket. |
| T8 | Release the axis only after a repeated traverse through the original speed band shows stable vibration, controlled housing temperature, and no renewed end float or noise at left support BX-FE1. Accept the repair when the report names the exact fixed-end boundary from locknut to bearing pair to seal path and ties it to the measured recovery. |
