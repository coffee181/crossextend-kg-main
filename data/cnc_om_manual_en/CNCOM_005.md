| Time step | O&M sample text |
|---|---|
| T1 | On Velorian MillCenter-900, record the complaint as spindle-drive thermal alarm after twenty to thirty minutes at speed, abnormal fan noise from the rear motor shroud, or visible fan blade damage found during inspection. Capture spindle speed, motor temperature, and the installed fan part number before any removal begins. |
| T2 | Isolate power and expose the spindle motor cooling fan, finger guard, fan connector, thermal-switch branch, rear shroud, and the encoder cable that shares the same upper housing. Keep the fan, guard, and cable route visible together because a correct fan can still fail closeout if the harness is later trapped against the rotating guard. |
| T3 | Inspect the removed area for dust packing, cracked blades, loose mounting studs, worn guard rub marks, and discoloration at the fan connector. Measure incoming fan supply voltage and fan current during a controlled test command if the old unit still rotates, because low airflow caused by weak supply should not be mistaken for a failed impeller. |
| T4 | Compare the replacement fan with the removed unit for frame depth, bolt circle, airflow direction, connector keying, and rotation arrow, then verify that the new fan clears the rear shroud and guard through the full blade arc. A visually similar fan with reversed airflow will clear the alarm briefly yet leave the motor hot under the next long spindle cycle. |
| T5 | Remove the old fan and clean the shroud, vent screen, and the lower pocket where dust accumulates below the motor housing. Inspect the rear vent screen and the nearby encoder cable sleeve so debris or a loose cable tie does not become a secondary blockage once the new fan is installed. |
| T6 | Install the replacement fan, route the harness through the grommet and the original clip path, and seat the connector fully into its bracketed receptacle. Confirm that the guard sits flat, the mounting screws pull the fan square against the shroud, and the harness does not cross the blade path or press on the encoder cable. |
| T7 | Re-energize only as needed to verify fan start, rotation direction, current draw, and airflow at the rear vent. Compare measured fan current and motor-surface temperature trend with the pre-repair values so the closeout depends on real cooling recovery rather than on the alarm merely staying quiet for a short time. |
| T8 | Hold the spindle in the complaint speed band long enough to reproduce the original thermal load while the rear shroud remains observable. Watch for guard vibration, connector heating, and whether airflow remains steady once the motor housing expands and the cable bundle settles into its normal running position. |
| T9 | After the thermal hold, inspect the guard screws, connector latch, vent screen, and cable clip again for movement or rubbing that appeared only with the fan in operation. This second inspection is required because several rear-motor fan jobs pass cold but fail after the first hot expansion cycle. |
| T10 | Release the machine only after the note lists removed and installed fan identities, measured supply and current values, final airflow direction, and the post-run motor temperature result. Accept the repair when the motor cooling boundary is restored from fan blade to connector to guard to vent path, with no renewed thermal alarm during the proven spindle duty cycle. |
