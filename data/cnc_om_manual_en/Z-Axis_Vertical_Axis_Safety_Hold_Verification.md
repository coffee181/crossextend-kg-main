| Time step | O&M sample text |
|---|---|
| T1 | On Kestrel VMC-845, begin the vertical-axis safety hold check by naming the recently serviced Z components, such as brake, counterbalance, or drive, because the hold test must match the repaired safety chain. Record the tested Z heights and the acceptable movement limit before any servo-off or hold test is started. |
| T2 | Prepare the machine in the prescribed safe state, verify mechanical support as required, and expose the brake housing, counterbalance pressure indication, ball-screw path, and the encoder or position reference used for movement judgment. Keep the safety chain and the mechanical load path visible together because hold failure can belong to either side. |
| T3 | Command the required hold state and measure whether the Z axis remains stationary, creeps, drops, or bounces at the tested height. Compare observed movement with brake command and counterbalance pressure at the same moment so the boundary can be placed on a brake path, a balance path, or a remaining mechanical load issue. |
| T4 | Repeat the hold check at the lower, middle, and upper test positions required by the service method because vertical stability often changes with head weight distribution and cylinder geometry. A good result at one height does not close a safety chain that still fails at another part of Z travel. |
| T5 | Inspect brake housing, counterbalance fittings, hose routing, ball-screw support, and the nearby guide surfaces after the first hold sequence, looking for leaks, drag, or witness movement that explain unstable behavior. This inspection ties the measured movement back to one real hardware path instead of leaving it as a generic did not hold observation. |
| T6 | If any correction was made, repeat the hold sequence under the same safe conditions and compare movement, pressure, and brake behavior with the original values. The machine cannot be released if the axis still approaches the movement limit even though the brake or counterbalance now appears normal at first glance. |
| T7 | Restore covers and supports to production position and run one final hold check in the fully assembled state because cable guides, covers, and hoses can alter the load path once the open service condition is closed. The fully assembled check is the true release boundary for a vertical safety chain. |
| T8 | Close the work only after the report names the tested hold condition, the measured Z movement at each required position, the exact acceptable boundary, and the specific brake or counterbalance path confirmed healthy. Accept the machine when the Z axis remains within the movement limit at all required heights with the repaired safety chain fully assembled and stable. |
