| T1 | On Kestrel SafetyPack-575, record whether the pyrofuse complaint is intermittent open, post-impact connector looseness, or a pyrofuse DTC after earlier body repair, and note any stored event that may have stressed the harness mechanically. Save the connector orientation before the first lock is moved. |
| T2 | Make the pack safe and expose the pyrofuse connector, CPA or secondary lock, terminal cavities, rear seal, wire exits, and first support clip while preserving the harness in its natural loaded shape. The connector face and the rear support need to be judged together because rear preload can pull a terminal out of retention. |
| T3 | Inspect the cavity face and rear seal for terminal recession, broken primary latch, incomplete CPA seating, or a seal that has started to walk out of its counterbore. A connector that looks clean at the face can still fail if the rear seal or support clip drags the wire set rearward. |
| T4 | Use the approved drag tool or mating pin to compare retention force on the suspect terminal against the neighboring cavity and record the difference at each position, noting whether the loss appears at initial engagement, mid-stroke, or final seated depth. Retention force belongs to a specific spring box and cavity geometry, not to the connector as a whole, so the comparison has to stay tied to one named terminal location. |
| T5 | If retention is weak, depin the terminal and inspect the spring box, conductor crimp, insulation crimp, and seal barrel for over-expansion, cracked plating, or wire movement under pull. Tie every observed defect back to the exact cavity number and support condition. |
| T6 | Correct only the failed spring box, CPA, rear seal, or support-clip side load, then repeat the drag and continuity proof with the connector locked and the harness returned to its final clip path. Confirm that the suspect cavity now matches the neighboring cavity in retention feel, that the rear seal remains fully seated in the counterbore, and that the measured result survives the restored preload at the first support without any renewed terminal walk-back. |
| T7 | Sign off this pyrofuse check only when the report names the exact connector cavity, terminal, seal, or support interface that was at fault and records the final retention or continuity outcome at that location together with the restored harness route. The release boundary is a pyrofuse connector that holds terminal position, CPA seating, rear-seal compression, and stable electrical state after covers and supports are fully restored and the branch is returned to its normal loaded geometry. |
