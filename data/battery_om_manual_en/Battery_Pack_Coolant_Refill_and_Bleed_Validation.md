| T1 | On Kestrel ThermalPack-619, record which branches were opened during service and what coolant volume was added before starting refill and bleed validation, then identify the highest bleed points on the pack branch. The fill record has to name the actual branch and the actual opened joints. |
| T2 | Fill through the specified point and confirm reservoir behavior while identifying the upstream and downstream bleed points that could trap air in the pack plates, such as the front manifold bleed screw, rear cold-plate high point, and upper degas line return. Keep the reservoir and the named bleed points in one evidence chain so the recovered volume and bubble release are tied to the actual pack branch that was opened. |
| T3 | Bleed each point in the correct order while monitoring pump command, bubble release, and branch temperature change because a quiet reservoir alone does not prove the pack plates are fully charged with coolant. Record which bleed point still emits foam or delayed liquid column, and note whether the delay appears at the front manifold point, rear high point, or degas return. |
| T4 | Once bubble release stops, command circulation and inspect all opened joints, quick connectors, and bleed screws for seepage under restored pressure and flow, including the manifold quick connector, bleed-seat washer, and any service-opened hose clamp line. A filled branch that leaks under circulation is not a valid recovery even if the bleed stream looked stable in static mode. |
| T5 | Compare inlet and outlet temperature response plus hose firmness after circulation to verify that the pack branch behaves as a filled hydraulic circuit rather than one still carrying compressible air pockets, and confirm the temperature drop or rise follows the same operating state used at intake. This comparison defines the hydraulic release boundary at branch level, not at reservoir level alone. |
| T6 | Finish the refill and bleed validation only when the record states fill amount, bleed points used, leak result, and the final thermal response that confirmed the branch was fully recovered, together with the final routed condition of clips and supports around the opened joints. The release boundary is a branch that circulates, vents, and seals correctly through the same hydraulic path that was opened during service and remains stable after full reassembly. |
