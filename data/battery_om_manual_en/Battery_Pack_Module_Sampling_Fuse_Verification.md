| T1 | On Novaris SamplePack-478, record the module row or sampling branch associated with the suspected sampling fuse and the symptom it created, such as lost voltage-measurement channel, missing temperature group, or no diagnostic access to one monitored section, before the holder is opened. Save the as-found fuse position and color witness at the holder so the later correction stays tied to the affected battery-sampling path. |
| T2 | Expose the sampling-fuse holder, fuse blades, terminal pair, cover, and the upstream and downstream sense branches without removing the fuse immediately, because the seated depth and holder color are part of the diagnosis. Keep both sides of the fuse path in one evidence chain from the BMS sampling feed toward the affected module branch rather than treating the holder as an isolated low-voltage part. |
| T3 | Measure continuity through the fuse and verify voltage or signal presence on both sides of the fuse path so an open branch is not misdiagnosed as a blown fuse when the upstream feed is actually absent. Record which side loses the expected value first. |
| T4 | Inspect the fuse blades, holder terminal grip, cover fit, and plastic body for heat, looseness, discoloration, or contamination that would create intermittent sampling loss even if the fuse element remains intact. A weak holder and an open fuse are different repair boundaries. |
| T5 | Compare the suspect holder with a healthy one on the same assembly where possible, paying attention to blade depth, terminal drag, and cover seating so the diagnosis is grounded in a real reference. This helps keep the correction on one holder path rather than on the whole sampling loom. |
| T6 | Finish the fuse-path verification only when the report states whether the fault belongs to the fuse element, fuse-holder terminal pair, upstream feed, or downstream sampling lead, and records continuity plus signal presence beside that hardware at the affected sampling branch. The release boundary is a fuse path that carries the required sampling signal or feed voltage through a stable holder with correct seating and no renewed heat, looseness, or channel loss at the repaired battery-monitoring circuit. |
