| T1 | On Aurex PackMount-506, record the exact undertray or side-bracket impact evidence and note whether the complaint is visible deformation, shield contact, or suspected load transfer into the pack mounting structure. Save the as-found bracket geometry before the first fastener moves. |
| T2 | Expose the impacted bracket, pack-case interface, fastener row, spacer blocks, adjacent shield edges, and any neighboring hose or harness path without prying the bracket away from its natural position. The direction of the hit is often readable only in the untouched geometry. |
| T3 | Inspect for bent flanges, cracked bends, shifted witness paint, crushed spacers, and new contact marks on the case that show whether the bracket absorbed the impact or pushed the load farther inward. Record which feature actually took the force. |
| T4 | Compare the bracket with the opposite side or an unaffected section so severity is judged against reference geometry rather than visual impression alone. This helps keep the diagnosis on one bracket ear, one spacer, or one fastener land. |
| T5 | Check whether the deformed bracket now loads a hose, harness, vent line, or shield edge, because secondary contact after the impact often becomes the next failure even if the bracket still appears attached. The secondary path has to be named separately from the primary bend. |
| T6 | Restore or classify only the affected bracket, spacer block, fastener land, or secondary contact condition, then recheck seating witness and local clearance after final loading because some offsets appear only once the neighboring hardware compresses normally. The bracket must prove stable in its installed stack, not only on a bench view. |
| T7 | Close the impact inspection only when the report names the exact damaged item, the direction and consequence of the deformation, any secondary contact it created, and the final loaded geometry at that location. The release boundary is a mounting path with stable fit, spacer support, and no unresolved transfer of load into the pack case or neighboring branches. |
