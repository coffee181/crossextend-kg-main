| Time step | O&M sample text |
|---|---|
| T1 | Start the enclosure-seal replacement on Arcline Logic-579 LR by reviewing the moisture complaint or prior leak history and checking the BMS enclosure perimeter first, so the new seal is not installed over a lid, flange, or pass-through defect that would immediately recreate the same leak path. Record which side or corner showed water tracks, because an upper left pass-through leak and a lower flange seep have different boundaries even though both wet the same enclosure. |
| T2 | Depower the controller, remove the BMS lid, and extract the old seal while tracking the original joint position, flattened sections, adhesive residue, and any corner where the gasket appears pinched, under-compressed, or damaged by previous service work. Keep the removed seal indexed so the final failure can still be tied to one joint, one bolt-boss transition, or one connector pass-through corner after the groove has been cleaned. |
| T3 | Clean the full seal groove, bolt-boss transitions, connector pass-through corners, and lid flange, then inspect for scratches, flange distortion, trapped dirt, previous seal scrap, or burrs; dry-fit the lid if needed to confirm flatness, dowel alignment, and even flange contact before the new gasket goes in. This preparation step should separate a bad gasket from a damaged sealing surface, because a rolled flange or raised burr will defeat any replacement seal installed over it. |
| T4 | Install the replacement enclosure seal with the joint placed away from the known water path, confirming that the gasket sits fully in the groove along straight runs and remains untwisted around the corners, bosses, and pass-through transitions where compression normally varies. Check that the seal height stays consistent around the connector opening and dowel points so no local high spot forces the lid to bridge over one section of the enclosure. |
| T5 | Refit the lid and tighten the fasteners in the intended cross pattern, watching the seal-compression line, lid-to-base gap, and pass-through corners so no one side closes early while another remains visibly high, then perform the specified splash or low-pressure leak confirmation on the enclosure boundary. The release check has to prove both uniform compression and a dry enclosure path at the original leak side rather than only a visually closed lid. |
| T6 | Release the controller area only after the work order includes the seal lot, joint location, flange condition, dowel alignment result, tightening sequence, pass-through check, original leak side, and final closure result for the BMS enclosure seal replacement. The accepted boundary is an enclosure that closes evenly, keeps the new gasket seated through every corner transition, and shows no renewed moisture path at the previously identified leak zone. |
