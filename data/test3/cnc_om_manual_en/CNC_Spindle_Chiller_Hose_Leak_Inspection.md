| T1 | On Kestrel VMC-850, record the spindle chiller complaint as coolant dripping from the rear column only during circulation, residue collecting on the lower cable tray, or coolant loss that appears after a sustained 8000 to 12000 rpm spindle hold. Note the as-received coolant level, spindle speed window, and the first visible drip path before any guard is removed. |
| T2 | Isolate the machine as required and expose spindle-chiller supply hose HC-S1, return hose HC-R1, worm-drive clamps CL-S1 and CL-R1, brass tube necks, chiller manifold block, P-clip PC-RC1 on the rear column, and the lower drip tray. Keep the upper outlet hose and lower return hose visible together so upstream and downstream wetting can be separated instead of being read as one large stain. |
| T3 | Trace the wet boundary from the lowest drip point back toward the upper hose bead, the clamp screw housing, the molded hose bend, or the manifold face, and document whether the stain runs front to rear along the cable tray or drops straight down from the hose neck. Preserve hose twist and clip position because a side-loaded branch often leaks only when the machine is in its final supported geometry. |
| T4 | Pressure-test the branch at 1.5 bar with the spindle stopped and compare the result with the dry reference hose on the opposite side of the chiller block. If branch HC-R1 stays dry under static pressure but wets the lower tray once the chiller pump runs, keep the evidence on vibration load, clamp position, and hose insertion depth rather than replacing the manifold on assumption. |
| T5 | Measure clamp position relative to the tube bead and check hose insertion depth at both the upper outlet neck and the lower return neck. A clamp that sits more than a few millimeters behind the bead or a hose that is pulled rearward by the P-clip can look seated while still opening a leak path once pump pulsation and spindle vibration act on the branch together. |
| T6 | Run the chiller pump and hold the spindle at the complaint speed with the rear guard still open, then watch the upper clamp, the molded bend, and the P-clip bracket through the full vibration event. If wetting starts only when the hose is tugged toward the rear column clip, isolate the fault boundary to the upper return-hose clamp and clip-to-bracket side load rather than to the manifold casting or the lower drain path. |
| T7 | Reposition the hose fully against the tube bead, rotate the clamp so the screw housing does not contact the column bracket, and restore the branch through the original clip path with a relaxed bend radius. Confirm that the hose is seated against the bead, the clamp band is centered, and the P-clip is holding the branch without twisting the upper neck sideways. |
| T8 | Release the machine only after a repeated pressure hold and a repeated high-speed circulation run leave the upper hose neck, lower return path, and drip tray dry. The accepted closeout is a named leak boundary at return-hose clamp CL-R1 or support path PC-RC1, with no renewed coolant trace from upper outlet to lower tray under the same speed window that created the complaint. |
