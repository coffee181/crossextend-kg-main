|---|---|
| T1 | On Novaris VMC-810, record the warm-up complaint as Y-axis vibration or chatter during reversal between 200 mm and 240 mm from home after approximately fifteen minutes of cycling, and note the feed rate, payload, and coolant state. This file is tied to the Y-axis ball-screw and rear support hardware, not to the spindle or the machine generally. |
| T2 | Run the same Y-axis motion window that produced the complaint and observe rear fixed bearing block BR-YR1, ball-screw shaft, nut housing, rear linear-guide trucks, way-cover segment, and lube manifold while the machine warms. Keep rear support and moving nut housing visible together so the vibration can be tied to one motion boundary instead of to background machine noise. |
| T3 | Measure vibration at rear bearing block BR-YR1 and compare it with the value at mid-travel or on X-axis reference condition, then note screw-surface temperature at sensor TS-YS when the complaint appears. A vibration peak that grows only after the rear support warms points to preload or support loss, while a constant vibration at all temperatures points elsewhere. |
| T4 | Inspect the rear locknut, bearing cap, way-cover leaf, guide-truck lubrication marks, and the screw-to-cover clearance for evidence of fretting, paint shift, dry track, or cover rub. Compare upper and lower way-cover contact marks so a loose support bearing is not confused with a cover leaf that is only grazing the screw near reversal. |
| T5 | Measure axial float or backlash at the Y-axis screw while the machine remains warm, then compare that value with the cold or accepted reference. If axial float at the rear support exceeds the expected band and vibration drops when the support is preloaded by hand, isolate the fault boundary to the rear fixed-bearing locknut or bearing-preload stack rather than to the nut housing or servo tuning. |
| T6 | Restore rear bearing preload, lock the nut, and verify that the support block sits squarely against the saddle casting with the lube line and way-cover segment returned to their original paths. Confirm that the cover leaf no longer touches the screw and that the rear guide trucks remain lubricated rather than dry at the same travel zone. |
| T7 | Repeat the warm-up cycle through the same feed range and reversal points, then compare hot vibration, screw temperature, and backlash with the pre-repair values. The corrected condition must hold through the actual motion window that created the complaint, not just during a short cold jog near home. |
| T8 | Inspect the rear bearing block, way-cover leaf, lube manifold fittings, and guide-truck area again after the repeated motion cycle because settling after travel often reveals a loose fastener or displaced cover segment. This second inspection confirms that the restored preload did not simply shift the symptom to an adjacent support part. |
| T9 | Close the work only after the record names the exact boundary, such as rear fixed-bearing preload at BR-YR1, locknut movement, cover interference, guide-truck lubrication loss, or no defect found at the checked support, and ties it to measured vibration and axial float. Accept the repair when Y-axis reversal remains smooth through the original warm motion window and the rear support hardware holds its corrected position. |
