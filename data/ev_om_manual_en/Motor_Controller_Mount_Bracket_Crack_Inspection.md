| T1 | On Kestrel MCX-471 AWD, document whether the complaint is a metallic knock on torque reversal, visible controller shake at idle, or repeat loosening of the side stay, and capture which side of the controller housing carries the suspect bracket before any fastener preload is disturbed. |
| T2 | Power the system down, expose the controller mount assembly, and identify the full load path that includes the cast aluminum bracket ear, steel side stay, rubber isolator, through-bolt sleeve, and chassis pad so the inspection covers more than the visible crack line alone. |
| T3 | Support the controller lightly to neutralize mount load, then inspect the bracket face, bolt heads, washer seats, and isolator compression witness. A crack at the ear root often pairs with shiny slip marks at the sleeve or a tilted washer that shows the bracket has been carrying side load. |
| T4 | Clean the suspect area and apply dye penetrant or magnified visual inspection across the ear radius, weld toe if present, and bolt-hole edge, extending the check onto the rear face where short cracks often continue beyond what is visible from the accessible side. |
| T5 | Measure bracket alignment against the chassis pad and examine the nearby coolant pipe, LV harness clip, and controller case ribs for contact marks, because a shifted mount can create both the crack and a secondary witness pattern on neighboring hardware. |
| T6 | Verify fastener condition by checking bolt shank straightness, thread engagement, sleeve fit in the isolator, and torque retention at each mounting point. Separate hardware looseness, seized sleeves, or collapsed rubber elements from a pure casting defect before deciding whether the bracket itself is the primary failed part. |
| T7 | Record the crack boundary in engineering terms, such as hairline crack from upper ear radius to bolt hole, through-crack at weld toe, or bracket intact with only paint crazing, and state whether the controller is safe to return to service or must remain quarantined for bracket replacement. |
| T8 | Finish the inspection only after the note links the crack geometry, affected bracket position, associated fastener or isolator condition, and any secondary contact marks on harness or coolant hardware, so the final sample reads like a real structural inspection rather than a generic bracket check. |
