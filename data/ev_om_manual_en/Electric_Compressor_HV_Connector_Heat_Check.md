| T3 | Inspect the connector front and rear for heat tint, latch damage, terminal recession, seal swelling, soot, or witness movement at the first clip point, because compressor motion and line vibration often show up first as backshell strain rather than as a visible open circuit. |

| T5 | Check the support bracket and nearby line clamps for preload on the connector body, confirming that refrigerant line movement or missing clip retention is not twisting the connector into a partial seat under load. |

| T7 | Reconnect the branch and hold the compressor through a realistic operating cycle, watching whether connector temperature, isolation status, and harness stability remain normal once shell vibration and line pressure have increased. |
