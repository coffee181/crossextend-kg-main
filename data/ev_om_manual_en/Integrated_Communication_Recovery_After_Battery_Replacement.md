| T1 | On Kestrel FusionDrive-446 EV, record the post-service complaint as telematics offline, ADAS unavailable, or repeated gateway communication faults after a 12 V battery replacement, and capture the new battery specification, the state of the IBS sensor connector, and whether the vehicle completes a normal wake-sleep-wake sequence. |
| T2 | Access the luggage-compartment electronics bay and expose the central gateway J533, the CAN splice pack S-CAN-2, fuse F29 for wake supply, ground G302, the telematics control unit connector, and the Intelligent Battery Sensor branch at the negative terminal, keeping the harnesses in their original routed position. |
| T3 | Verify the recovery baseline by checking battery voltage under wake, terminal 30 supply at J533, ignition wake input, CAN high and CAN low resting voltage, and the presence of module chatter on the bus before any connector is disturbed, because a quiet network with no wake command is a different condition from a waking network that drops out under load. |
| T4 | Inspect the J533 connector latches, splice-pack seating, F29 fit, G302 eyelet, and the IBS plug for partial engagement, bent pins, trapped trim, or missing CPA features that may have been disturbed while the battery tray, hold-down, or rear trim were removed. |
| T5 | Run a controlled sleep-wake cycle and observe whether gateway startup, telematics registration, and ADAS network participation return together or whether one branch remains absent, then use that sequence to separate a single bad splice or wake-line issue from a broader low-voltage supply problem. |
| T6 | If communication still collapses, isolate the fault as J533 connector seating, splice-pack S-CAN-2 contact loss, F29 wake-feed interruption, G302 resistance, IBS branch disturbance, or no defect in the rear bay with the issue migrating farther downstream to a specific module leg. |
| T7 | Close the inspection only after the wake-sleep-wake cycle is repeatable with the rear-bay harnesses, trim, and battery-related branches back in installed position, the restored network boundary is named explicitly, and the record links that boundary to the measured supply, bus, and module-recovery evidence gathered after the battery replacement. |
