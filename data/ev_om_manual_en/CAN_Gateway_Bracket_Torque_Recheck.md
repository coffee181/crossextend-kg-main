| T1 | On Novaris NetBridge-685 AWD, log the complaint as intermittent gateway dropouts over bumps after cowl or dash-beam service, then capture current U-code status, battery voltage, and whether the fault can be triggered by light body shake at idle. Keep the intake note tied to the front cowl gateway bracket area so the later vibration and retention checks stay on the same suspected support path. |
| T2 | Remove the upper cowl cover and the left dash-side trim to expose the gateway bracket plate, the XG14 connector backshell, the upper-right M6 stud, the lower isolator grommet, the shield-drain eyelet, and the harness clip mounted on the dash beam. Inspect whether the bracket ear is seated against the beam or whether a visible upper gap remains between bracket and mounting land. |
| T3 | Check witness marks on the stud washer, bracket ear, and harness clip before tightening anything, then compare the upper-right bracket gap with the lower mounting point. A gap larger than about 2 mm at the upper ear, a rotated shield-drain eyelet, or a backshell that lifts when the harness is pushed rearward usually points to bracket load loss rather than an internal connector defect. |
| T4 | Measure breakout torque on the upper-right M6 stud and compare it with the specified 8 N m target, then monitor CAN status while lightly loading the bracket by hand. If the network stabilizes when the bracket is held firmly against the dash beam, while CAN resistance remains near 60 ohms and no cavity damage is seen at XG14, the moving bracket becomes the primary fault path. |
| T5 | Loosen the lower support just enough to reseat the bracket squarely, restore the shield-drain eyelet to its original stack position, and torque the upper stud and lower mount in sequence so the bracket is seated against both lands without twisting. Reclip the harness so the cable load is held by the beam clip and not by the XG14 backshell. |
| T6 | Perform a tap test and vibration check with the trim still open, then release only after the bracket gap is closed, stud torque is confirmed, the shield drain remains flat, and no gateway dropout returns during body shake. The fault boundary is accepted as loose gateway bracket retention if the symptom is gone with the bracket restored and no connector or module issue remains. |
