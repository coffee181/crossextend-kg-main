| T1 | Before removing the side module-access bracket on Kestrel E-Motion-344 AWD, confirm which battery module is being accessed and mark the lift-table support points, side bracket fastener positions, shim locations, and surrounding harness retainers. Tie the opening record to the actual module-access boundary and the original shim map so the bracket can be returned to the same load path later. |
| T2 | Isolate the battery, support the pack rail with the lift table, and remove the side shield so the side bracket, rail flange, bracket feet, shim stack, captive nuts, and adjacent module-access opening are all visible and mechanically supported. |
| T3 | Loosen the bracket fasteners in the planned sequence while maintaining pack support, and note any bracket spring-back, shim migration, paint witness break, or change in rail-to-bracket gap that shows the bracket was carrying uneven side load. This load change is the main clue for whether the bracket was already storing side preload before it was released. |
| T4 | Remove the side bracket fully, keeping every bolt, washer, and shim indexed by position, then inspect the bracket feet, rail flange, module-access opening, and captive-nut condition for fretting, distortion, elongated holes, or evidence of prior forced installation. A distorted foot, pulled captive nut, or shifted shim seat has to be named now before the bracket is installed again around a new service task. |
| T5 | Check that the opened module-access area is protected immediately, and verify that no loose hardware, dirt, gasket scrap, or detached clip pad can fall into the battery cavity while the bracket remains off for the next service stage. The opening must stay clean and mechanically stable because the next stage depends on an unchanged cavity edge and rail flange condition. |
| T6 | Tag the removed hardware as battery side module-access bracket, record bolt order, shim order, and support condition, and state clearly whether the later reinstallation must follow a specific center-out or left-to-right sequence to reestablish alignment. |
| T7 | Leave the bracket removed only after the access opening, nearby harnesses, and rail flange are protected, and hand the pack onward with the bracket hardware kit, shim map, and side-load observations attached to the module-access work record. The handoff note should still let the next technician restore the same bracket stack and support path without guessing the original shim and load order. |
