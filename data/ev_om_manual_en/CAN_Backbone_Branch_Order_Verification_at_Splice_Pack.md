| T1 | On Velorian CANLink-684 Plus, record the complaint as no communication with the rear charge ECU after sill-harness work, intermittent charger wake failure, or U-code return immediately after low-voltage reconnection. Note whether the network sleeps normally, which modules are missing on the scan, and whether the failure changes when the left sill harness is flexed near the splice-pack location. |
| T2 | Open the left A-pillar lower trim and the left sill harness channel to expose splice pack S218, the upstream gateway branch, the rear charge ECU branch, the telematics branch, and the shield-drain splice that is mounted on the sill reinforcement. Keep the branch labels, tape flags, and cavity marks visible because branch order mistakes are usually hidden once the loom is rewrapped. |
| T3 | Photograph and record the as-found order of each branch entering S218, including wire color, circuit ID, cavity position, and the upstream-to-downstream route from A-pillar to rear quarter. Pay attention to whether the rear charge ECU pair is routed through the center clip with the telematics pair, because cross-routing at the clip often accompanies cross-connection inside the splice pack. |
| T4 | Open the splice-pack cover and inspect the secondary lock, terminal seating height, and cavity identification on both CAN-H and CAN-L legs. Compare the terminal position on the rear charge ECU leg with the neighboring telematics leg, and verify that the shield drain remains connected to the correct eyelet rather than trapped under the cover tab or shifted to the wrong side of the pack. |
| T5 | Measure continuity from the upstream gateway branch to each downstream leg one circuit at a time, then compare those results with the printed circuit IDs on the loom labels. The correct rear charge ECU leg should show the lowest continuity path to the rear quarter connector and the same pair orientation listed in the service map, while a swapped leg usually shows good continuity but lands at the wrong downstream module. |
| T6 | With the pack still open, measure backbone resistance at the upstream side and then at the rear charge ECU connector after each leg is confirmed. If overall CAN resistance stays near 60 ohms but the rear charge ECU still disappears while the telematics branch unexpectedly carries the same pair identity, isolate the fault boundary to cross-connected branch order inside splice pack S218 rather than a module failure or a broken rear harness. |
| T7 | Restore the correct branch order one leg at a time, making sure CAN-H and CAN-L enter the intended cavities, the secondary lock sits fully home, and the shield drain is routed through its original lower guide. Reclip the branches so the upstream gateway pair stays in the forward clip and the rear charge ECU pair remains routed rearward through the sill channel without twisting around the telematics branch. |
| T8 | Reconnect low-voltage power, let the network wake, and verify module presence, sleep recovery, and charge ECU communication while gently loading the left sill harness by hand. The release condition is not only restored communication but also stable branch identity under movement, normal backbone resistance, and no wake disturbance when the sill clip and splice-pack cover are pressed or tapped. |
| T9 | Close the sill channel and lower trim only after the work record names the fault boundary as incorrect splice-pack branch order or confirms that branch order was ruled out. Accept the repair when S218 shows correct cavity order, the rear charge ECU branch is confirmed on the intended downstream route, and the network remains stable through wake and sleep. |
