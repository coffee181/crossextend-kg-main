| T1 | On Trion PTC-475 LR, define the heating complaint as weak output, delayed warm-up, or heater cycling off after initial operation, and capture battery state of charge, reservoir level, ambient temperature, and the inlet and outlet hose temperatures before service access begins. |
| T2 | Safe the high-voltage heater branch and expose the PTC heater can, the power connector, the control plug, the inlet hose, the outlet hose, the local bleed point, and the ground stud with the branch still in its natural support layout. |
| T3 | Confirm coolant-side readiness first by checking pump command, flow noise, outlet-hose temperature lag, and reservoir behavior, because a PTC heater with correct current but air trapped at the outlet can imitate an electrical failure from the driver seat. |
| T4 | Inspect the power and control connectors for rear-seal issues, latch travel, terminal discoloration, and ground-eyelet clamp condition, then compare those findings with the thermal side so one dry electrical result is not treated as full proof while the hydraulic path remains compromised. |
| T5 | Command the heater through a complete activation cycle and measure current draw, voltage presence, and hose temperature rise together, paying attention to whether the branch starts correctly and then drops out once coolant demand or connector temperature increases. |
| T6 | If heating is unstable, separate the fault as power-side interruption, control-enable loss, trapped-air branch, no-flow condition, or internal heater collapse by comparing command status, outlet response, and bleed behavior at the same moment in the cycle. |
| T7 | Repeat the heating event after any bleed or connector correction and hold the branch long enough to cover stable operation, not only the first activation pulse that can hide intermittent failures. |
| T8 | Release the heater branch only after the record states the verified fault boundary, the measured current and thermal response, and the accepted final condition of the power connector, control plug, outlet hose, and ground stud, with stable heater operation confirmed through the repeat hold period and no renewed drop-out after the initial activation pulse. |
