| T2 | Safe the low-voltage side of the battery-management branch and expose the pack bulkhead connector XBM-34, the CAN high and CAN low pair, the shield-drain braid termination, splice pack S-BMS-CAN, the wake line, and the first support clamp on the pack-lid harness so the signal pair and its shielding path are visible end to end. |
| T3 | Inspect the twisted-pair lay, shield-drain crimp, braid clamp, secondary lock, and seal land at XBM-34 for untwist beyond the connector shoulder, loose braid strands, moisture residue, or clamp rotation that could turn the shield drain into an intermittent noise source rather than a solid reference. |
| T4 | Disconnect the bulkhead and splice-pack interfaces and inspect terminal height, pin drag, rear-seal compression, and braid termination depth, because a bus pair with correct continuity can still lose noise immunity if the shield drain or one terminal is only partially seated. |
| T5 | Measure bus resistance and common-mode behavior with the branch static, then repeat the check while flexing the pack-lid harness at the shield clamp and the first support point so a hidden shield-drain fracture is separated from a connector-face contact issue. |
| T6 | Run the vehicle through the operating condition that originally triggered the dropout, such as a wake event or a high-current charge session, and compare live BMS communication stability with the physical state of the shield termination and the nearby wake line. |
| T7 | If communication remains unstable, compare the XBM-34 branch with the adjacent battery-management branch or known-good reference harness to determine whether the defect lies in the shield drain, the splice pack, the bulkhead seal, the wake-line coupling, or the BMS-side connector itself. |
| T8 | Restore the branch with the exact clamp order and bend radius required by the harness drawing, making sure the twisted pair remains supported and the shield braid is not left carrying mechanical load from the pack-lid movement. |
| T9 | Repeat the communication check through another wake or charge cycle after the branch is restored so the acceptance decision reflects both electrical stability and mechanical retention in the final routed condition. |
| T10 | Finish the record only after the bus fault is tied to one hardware boundary such as XBM-34 terminal seating, shield-drain crimp loss, splice-pack oxidation, wake-line coupling, bulkhead moisture, or no defect found under the reproduced condition, and after the repeated wake or charge cycle confirms stable BMS communication with the harness restored in final routed position. |
