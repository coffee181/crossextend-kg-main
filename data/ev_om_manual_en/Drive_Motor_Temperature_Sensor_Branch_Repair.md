| T1 | On Kestrel TorqueLine-389 EV, capture the derate message, freeze-frame temperature value, and whether the stator temperature reading is fixed high, fixed low, or intermittently open, then compare it with inverter temperature and coolant temperature so the repair starts from a believable thermal symptom. |
| T2 | After high-voltage isolation, remove the rear drive-unit upper cover and free the clipped sub-harness that carries the stator temperature sensor branch, exposing the two-core 0.5 mm2 TXL wires, Sumitomo TS-090 two-way connector, DR-25 sleeve, and grommet exit from the motor housing. |
| T3 | Follow the sensor branch from the housing grommet to the main harness and note every rub point at the inverter stay, P-clip, and sharp casting rib, because copper fracture 40 to 80 mm behind the connector and green corrosion at the rear seal are both common failure locations on this layout. |
| T4 | Measure sensor resistance at the motor-side connector, then measure again through the controller-side harness. A large difference or an unstable reading during wiggle testing will separate a failed thermistor pellet from an open conductor or poor terminal crimp in the service branch. |
| T5 | Open the loom only at the damaged section, identify the exact broken conductor or corroded span, and cut back to bright copper while preserving enough length to keep the branch stress-free when the connector is reinstalled on the housing bracket. |
| T6 | Build the repair with two sealed open-barrel butt splices, stagger the joints by at least 25 mm, crimp with the approved 0.35 to 0.5 mm2 die, recover adhesive heat-shrink fully around each splice, and replace any swollen rear seal or loose TS-090 terminal instead of reusing suspect pieces. |
| T7 | Rewrap the branch with PET cloth tape inside the protected section, add a fresh abrasion sleeve where the loom crosses the inverter stay, and restore the P-clip orientation so the repaired wires leave the grommet with a natural bend rather than a tight reverse curl. |
| T8 | Verify the repaired circuit by checking stable sensor resistance cold, repeating a wiggle test at the connector rear and splice zone, and confirming the live temperature value now tracks workshop ambient instead of jumping to implausible extremes. |
| T9 | Close the record with the actual repaired hardware named as stator temperature sensor pigtail, TS-090 terminal pair, rear seal, splice section behind the motor grommet, or harness rub point at the inverter stay, so the extracted fault entity is specific and reproducible. |
