| T1 | On Kestrel FusionDrive-468 LR, begin the battery-pack closeout by identifying every item that was opened inside the service-disconnect bay, including the manual service disconnect receptacle, the orange insulating barrier, the interlock lead, the warning labels, and the stamped service cover that must be restored before pack release. Keep the closeout tied to this exact bay boundary so no unrelated pack-safe status is used as a substitute for disconnect-bay completion. |
| T2 | Inspect the service-disconnect receptacle, CPA feature, interlock pins, cover-seal land, and barrier tabs for burrs, cracked plastic, missing fasteners, or seal damage, because any distortion in this area can prevent the disconnect from seating fully even when the lever appears latched from outside the cover. Read both the latch window and the pin entry geometry so a false-latched handle is separated from a genuinely seated disconnect. |
| T3 | Check that each warning label is legible, fully bonded, and positioned on the correct cover face, and confirm that no internal note, torque marker, or temporary tag from the repair process remains inside the service bay where it could be trapped under the barrier or cover. This is a real closeout condition, not cosmetic cleanup, because trapped tags and lifted labels can interfere with barrier seating or mislead the next service event. |
| T4 | Reinstall the service disconnect and verify mechanical latch feel, interlock continuity, and correct lever travel, then compare those results with the expected pack-safe state before the outer cover is closed so a mis-seated disconnect is found while the bay is still accessible. Confirm that the CPA reaches full position and that interlock continuity stays stable when light hand pressure is applied at the handle and receptacle surround. |
| T5 | Fit the orange barrier and stamped cover in sequence, confirming screw count, seal compression, barrier overlap, and the absence of trapped harness, debris, or label corners that could compromise insulation distance or future re-entry to the disconnect bay. The barrier has to sit flat under the cover without one corner lifting, because partial overlap at the disconnect opening changes both protection distance and future service access. |
| T6 | Repeat the disconnect-bay continuity and latch confirmation after the barrier and cover are fully installed, verifying that the final closed geometry does not change lever seating, interlock continuity, or cover seal alignment compared with the open-bay check. The accepted condition is a bay that remains mechanically and electrically correct after closure, not only before it. |
| T7 | Release the pack closeout only after the record names the disconnect status, interlock result, barrier condition, label condition, cover-fastener outcome, and final closed-bay continuity result together, making the service-disconnect bay traceable as a complete safety boundary rather than a simple cover installation. |
