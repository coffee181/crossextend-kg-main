|---|---|
| T1 | On Novaris 12V Rail-304 EV, record the complaint as intermittent terminal 30 drop, slow wake-up, or repeated low-voltage reset during high accessory load. Capture open-circuit battery voltage, the customer load condition that triggers the reset, and whether the event appears during blower and defrost demand, because this work order is focused on the positive distribution stud stack rather than on the battery itself. |
| T2 | Isolate the 12 V source and expose the left-apron power distribution stud assembly, including the battery feed lug, the current-sensor shunt pad, the heater feed lug, the flat washer, the serrated washer, the nyloc nut, the insulator cap, and the cable support bracket mounted just below the stud. Keep the as-found hardware order visible before any nut is removed. |
| T3 | Inspect the stack from stud base to top, checking whether each copper lug is seated flat, whether the serrated washer is biting the intended conductive surface, and whether the insulator cap has been crushed against a lug edge. Compare the witness marks on the stud and nut with the expected order, because a reversed washer or a trapped lug often leaves an offset compression ring on the copper pad. |
| T4 | Apply an 80 A accessory load with the assembly still intact and measure voltage drop across the stud stack from the battery feed lug to the downstream heater or PDU lug. A healthy stack should stay within a few tens of millivolts, while an incorrect lug or washer order often shows a much larger drop and a localized temperature rise at the nut or upper lug face. |
| T5 | Disassemble the joint in service order and inspect both lug faces, the shunt pad, the stud shoulder, and the washers for burnishing, oxide, or imprint mismatch. Pay attention to whether the serrated washer was installed between painted or insulated surfaces instead of between the nut and the top conductive lug, because that mistake creates preload without real electrical contact. |
| T6 | Define the fault boundary only after the hardware stack is compared with the intended order from stud base upward. If the battery feed lug, shunt pad, and heater or PDU lug are healthy but the washer sequence or lug order is wrong at the stud, isolate the fault to the stud hardware order and not to the battery, the fuse block, or the downstream cable route. |
| T7 | Rebuild the stack in the correct sequence, seat each lug flat on the lower contact surface, restore the cable-to-bracket support so the upper lug is not pulled sideways, and torque the nut to the specified value without rotating the lower shunt pad. Confirm that the insulator cap clears the nut and that the cable bend begins after the support bracket rather than at the stud face. |
| T8 | Repeat the 80 A load check and release the vehicle only after voltage drop is restored to a normal level, the stud temperature remains stable, and the reset complaint no longer appears under the original accessory demand. The accepted closeout is a named hardware-order fault at the distribution stud with corrected stack sequence and verified electrical recovery. |
