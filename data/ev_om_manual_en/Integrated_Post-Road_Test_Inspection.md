| Time step | O&M sample text |
|---|---|
| T1 | On Aurex CrossFlow-721 Plus, begin the post-road-test review without shutting the vehicle down, recording whether the original complaint was a rear drive-unit overtemperature, a coolant odor after load, or a traction warning that returned only after several acceleration and regen events. Capture rear inverter temperature, battery coolant temperature, and any fresh warning or message while the hardware is still heat soaked. |
| T2 | Open the rear service hatch and the underbody inspection panel so the rear coolant manifold outlet barb, the outlet hose clamp, the resolver connector, the breather hose, the subframe support bracket, and the right-side cooler line can be inspected in their hot operating position. Do not disturb dried mist or fresh wetting on the undertray until the first leak or rub origin is identified. |
| T3 | Inspect the hot-side evidence along the upstream manifold-to-hose joint and then along the downstream hose-to-cooler path, noting whether residue begins at the clamp screw, the barb bead, the hose cut edge, or farther rearward at the support bracket. Compare that path with the resolver connector and breather hose condition so thermal seepage is not confused with unrelated splash or vent discharge. |
| T4 | Measure inlet and outlet hose temperatures, pump current, and rear inverter temperature while the vehicle idles after the drive, then compare those values with the pre-drive record. The relationship between upstream outlet temperature and downstream cooler return matters more than one isolated number, because a hot manifold with a cool return indicates different failure logic than a uniform temperature rise across the entire branch. |
| T5 | Isolate the boundary by naming the first abnormal point in the hot system chain. If seepage starts at the rear manifold outlet clamp while the cooler line, breather hose, and resolver connector stay dry, keep the fault on the outlet clamp or hose cut face; if no seepage exists but inverter temperature remains high with poor temperature drop across the cooler path, push the boundary downstream toward restricted flow. |
| T6 | Correct the found condition, hold the vehicle in a hot soak, and then repeat a shorter load cycle or service-mode torque event before the system cools completely. The release check must confirm that the rear manifold outlet, the cooler line support bracket, and the resolver connector remain stable under the same thermal expansion that produced the original complaint. |
| T7 | Close the service panels only after the post-road-test record names the exact hot boundary and shows recovery in both physical evidence and measured behavior. Accept the repair when the hot soak produces no fresh coolant trace, the rear inverter temperature follows a normal decay path, and the outlet-to-cooler branch remains dry and correctly supported. |
