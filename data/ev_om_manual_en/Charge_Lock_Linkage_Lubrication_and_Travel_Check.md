| T1 | On Lynxar ChargePort-357 Plus, record the charge complaint as connector lock not engaging, plug stuck after charging, or repeated lock-actuator timeout, and note whether the problem occurs on AC, DC, or both inlet use cases. |
| T2 | Remove the rear inlet trim and expose the lock actuator, the steel linkage rod, the latch pawl, the torsion spring, the microswitch bracket, the TE rear connector, and the inlet-seal carrier so the full lock path can be inspected from motor output to pin engagement. |
| T3 | Cycle the actuator with the service routine and watch the linkage travel, checking whether the rod reaches full stroke, the latch pawl centers on the plug notch, the torsion spring returns the mechanism cleanly, and the microswitch changes state at the expected point in travel. |
| T4 | Inspect the linkage pivots, retaining clips, actuator mounting screws, pawl nose, and seal carrier for dry pivots, bent rod geometry, missing clip retention, witness marks from trim interference, or seal distortion that shifts the lock path off center. |
| T5 | Disconnect the linkage from the actuator and move the pawl by hand to determine whether the drag is in the motor and gearbox, in the rod path, or in the latch pocket inside the inlet body. |
| T6 | Check the TE connector and actuator supply while commanding lock and unlock, because a weak motor stroke caused by voltage loss can look like a mechanical jam when the linkage is actually free. |
| T7 | Reassemble the linkage in the correct clip and spring orientation, then repeat the full lock and unlock cycle with a plug inserted so the mechanism is judged under the same geometry it sees during customer use rather than unloaded in the open position. |
| T8 | Inspect the final position of the pawl, the switch state, and the plug release feel after several repeat cycles, making sure the lock engages positively and releases without sticking, overshoot, or partial return. |
| T9 | Finish the record only after the boundary is named as actuator voltage loss, gearbox weakness, bent linkage rod, pawl interference, torsion-spring issue, trim contact, or normal mechanism operation under repeated inserted-plug cycles, and after the final accepted state confirms full lock engagement, correct switch transition, and clean manual and powered release on repeated inserted-plug checks. |
