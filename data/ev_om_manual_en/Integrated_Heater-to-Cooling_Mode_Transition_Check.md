| T1 | On Trion SystemSync-802 AWD, record the complaint as unstable changeover from cabin heating to battery cooling, delayed valve response, or battery loop temperature remaining high after the cabin heat request is removed. Note ambient temperature, cabin setpoint, battery inlet temperature, and whether the complaint appears during the first transition of the day or only after the thermal module is already warm. |
| T2 | Expose the thermal module so the PTC heater connector XH22, the three-way valve V3, coolant pump P2, the battery-chiller inlet hose, the outlet NTC, and the upper degas line are visible from heater outlet to downstream chiller return. Keep the inlet and outlet hoses separated visually, because crossed routing or a bracketed hose twist can mimic a control fault during the mode change. |
| T3 | Inspect connector seating, hose routing, valve bracket position, and clamp orientation before any command is issued. Pay particular attention to whether the outlet hose is held by the bracket clip below V3, whether the degas line rises continuously to the reservoir, and whether XH22 is fully locked with no secondary-latch lift at the upper edge. |
| T4 | Command cabin-heating mode first and log the thermal chain at steady state, including PTC current, pump current, heater outlet temperature, and battery-loop inlet temperature. This establishes whether the upstream heater path is healthy before the transition begins, because a weak heater or a weak pump can create a false valve diagnosis. |
| T5 | Remove the cabin heat request and command the system into battery-cooling mode while watching V3 position feedback, pump response, and the temperature change from heater outlet to chiller inlet. A normal transition should show the heater contribution dropping, the valve shifting to the cooling path, and the downstream battery-chiller inlet temperature falling in a controlled way rather than staying tied to the upstream heater outlet. |
| T6 | Compare upstream and downstream temperatures across the transition and, if necessary, command V3 directly while the hoses remain exposed. If PTC current falls but the downstream hose and battery-chiller inlet remain hot with little change in valve feedback, isolate the fault boundary to V3 spool movement, valve connector XH22 support, or outlet-side hose routing instead of blaming the BMS request path. |
| T7 | Correct the found hardware condition, then repeat the heating-to-cooling changeover twice with the thermal module still open. Confirm that pump current stays stable, the upper degas line remains free of fresh air release, and the outlet NTC follows the expected drop once V3 routes flow away from the heater branch. |
| T8 | Hold the system in battery-cooling mode after the second transition and inspect the full heater-to-chiller path for renewed hose twist, clamp movement, or connector lift at XH22. The release boundary is not just a successful command change but a stable downstream temperature response and a physically stable valve, pump, and hose assembly from upper module to lower return. |
| T9 | Reinstall the thermal-module covers and close the work order only after the record names the exact fault boundary and the accepted recovery state. Accept the repair when cabin-heating demand can be removed, V3 shifts cleanly, the downstream battery-cooling path stabilizes, and no abnormal temperature hold remains between the PTC outlet and the battery-chiller inlet. |
