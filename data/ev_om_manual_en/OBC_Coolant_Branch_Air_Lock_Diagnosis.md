| T1 | On Novaris ChargeCore-11, note whether the no-charge event follows coolant service, low reservoir level, or extended parking, and capture live data for OBC inlet temperature, OBC outlet temperature, pump speed command, and charge power taper before the cooling branch is disturbed. |
| T2 | Expose the OBC coolant loop from the inlet hose to the outlet hose, including the bleed screw, high-point hose bend, quick connectors, and reservoir return line, and record hose temperatures by touch or infrared before venting anything, because trapped air often shows a sharp temperature split across the charger body. |
| T3 | Confirm the reservoir level and pump operation first. A dry reservoir or a silent pump can mimic an air lock, while an actual air pocket usually leaves the pump running but the charger plate cool only on the lower half or only near the inlet neck. |
| T4 | With the branch cold enough to handle safely, crack the OBC bleed screw or designated bleed fitting and observe whether a solid coolant column appears immediately or whether the branch spits foam and intermittent bubbles that confirm trapped air at the local high point. |
| T5 | Massage the upper hose bend and cycle the electric pump if service mode allows it, then watch the clear return tube or bleed hose for bubble release. This step matters because small air pockets often move only when flow and hose orientation are changed together. |
| T6 | Compare the OBC branch temperature rise and hose firmness before and after bleeding, making sure the outlet hose warms progressively and the charge session no longer derates early, which distinguishes a resolved air lock from a blocked cold plate or a weak pump. |
| T7 | Inspect the quick-connector latches, hose routing, and any recent clamp work that could have introduced the air pocket, especially at the highest hose loop above the charger, because a slightly loose connector can ingest air on cooldown without leaving an obvious external coolant leak. |
| T8 | Close the diagnosis only after the record says whether the root cause was trapped air at the OBC high-point hose, low reservoir after prior service, pump non-operation, or another cooling fault, and includes the before-and-after temperature behavior that proved the conclusion. |
