| T1 | On Lynxar WarmFlow-419 AWD, record the complaint as heater inoperative, intermittent no-heat after soak, or PTC disable under load, and save the DTC state together with battery voltage, pack state of charge, reservoir level, and the initial heater inlet and outlet temperatures. |
| T2 | Safe the heater high-voltage circuit and expose the PTC module, the HVA280-2P power connector, the MX150 control plug, the heater housing ground stud, the inlet hose, the outlet hose, and the nearby support bracket with the branch still in its as-found routed position. |
| T3 | Check whether the problem belongs to electrical supply or coolant exchange by confirming pump command, hose temperature split, and reservoir behavior before any connector is unplugged, because a heater with current but no flow is a different failure chain from a heater that never receives enable or power. |
| T4 | Inspect the HVA280 latch, CPA, rear seal, terminal cavities, and the washer stack at the ground stud for soot, looseness, heat tint, or witness movement, then compare those findings with the MX150 control connector and its low-voltage seal line. |
| T5 | Command heater activation and measure high-voltage availability, low-voltage enable, current draw, and outlet-hose warm-up rate in the same run so the diagnosis stays tied to one operating condition rather than to separate electrical and thermal guesses. |
| T6 | If power and enable are correct but no heat follows, trace trapped air, blocked flow, or internal heater failure by watching outlet response, bleed behavior, and pump sound together. If enable is missing or unstable, narrow the fault to the control plug, ground stud, interlock chain, or power connector boundary. |
| T7 | Repeat the test through a sustained heat request and not only the first energization pulse, because some PTC faults appear only after connector temperature rises or coolant demand increases. |
| T8 | Inspect the surrounding mounts and supports before closure, making sure the branch routing, connector backshell angle, and hose support do not preload the power connector or hide a repeat chafe point near the bracket edge. |
| T9 | Close the diagnosis only after the record names the actual failed boundary as HVA280 seating, MX150 control path, ground-stud resistance, trapped-air heater branch, no-flow outlet circuit, or internal PTC element, and ties that boundary to both measured current behavior and thermal response, with the final release state recorded as stable heater current and repeatable outlet-hose warm-up under the same demand if a corrective action was completed. |
