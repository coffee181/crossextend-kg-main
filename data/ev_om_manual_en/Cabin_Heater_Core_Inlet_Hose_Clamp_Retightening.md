| T2 | Cool the system to a safe handling state, remove the lower cowl trim and local insulation panel, and expose the heater-core inlet tube, the 19 mm EPDM inlet hose, the constant-tension clamp, the aluminum bulkhead stub, the foam anti-chafe sleeve, and the nearby harness branch without changing the hose clocking or support load. |
| T3 | Clean and dry the area before disturbing the joint, then inspect the underside of the tube bead, the clamp ear position, the hose end witness line, and the foam sleeve for crusted coolant, fresh wetting, hose-liner swelling, or signs that seepage has been wicking along the harness and making the leak look larger than the actual source. |
| T4 | Apply a controlled pressure-hold test at the specified thermal-loop value and watch the heater-core inlet joint with a mirror and task light, checking whether the first moisture appears at the clamp gap, under the tube bead, from the quick-connector seal, or from an upstream bleed point that runs down onto the inlet hose. |
| T5 | If the static pressure hold remains dry, repeat the check with the pump commanded on and the coolant warmed, because some inlet-joint leaks open only when the EPDM hose softens and the aluminum tube expands, while a damaged O-ring or corroded bead often leaks even before circulation begins. |
| T6 | Isolate the boundary by comparing clamp retention, hose-end elasticity, tube-bead corrosion, quick-connector O-ring condition, and the wetting path from adjacent components, so the record distinguishes true inlet-joint seepage from runoff caused by the heater bleed screw, upper hose, or bulkhead drain path. |
| T7 | Restore the clamp position, foam sleeve, and branch support exactly as required, then verify that the hose remains dry through a short heat-soak and cooldown cycle and that no coolant is traveling along the nearby harness branch or trim edge after the localized check is complete. |
| T8 | Close the inspection only after the note names the leak source as clamp set loss, hardened hose end, corroded heater-core bead, quick-connector seal failure, upstream runoff, or no fault found, and ties that conclusion to the pressure value, temperature condition, and dry-check result recorded at the heater-core inlet joint. |
