| T1 | For Helion DCDC-3K0, record the low-voltage symptom in operating terms, such as battery lamp on, 12 V bus sag during READY, repeated low-battery wake failures, or unstable cabin loads, and note the battery state of charge so the converter check is not confused with a simply discharged auxiliary battery. Capture whether the complaint appears only during high accessory load or even at idle, because that distinction helps separate current-capacity loss from a complete no-output condition. |
| T2 | Make the high-voltage side safe enough for access, expose the converter output stud, ground strap, remote-sense wires, main low-voltage fuse, and battery feed cable, and inspect the as-found nut witness and insulation boots before any meter lead is attached. |
| T3 | Measure auxiliary-battery voltage at rest, then measure again at the converter output stud and at the battery positive terminal during READY, because the voltage difference between those two points often separates a weak converter from a high-resistance cable or fuse link. Record the drop on both the positive side and the ground return side so the loss is not incorrectly assigned to the converter when the strap or fuse path is responsible. |
| T4 | Apply a controlled low-voltage load such as blower, rear defogger, and coolant pump, and watch whether the converter regulates the 12 V bus to the expected band or whether the voltage collapses only under load, which indicates that current capability rather than open-circuit output is the issue. |
| T5 | Check the remote-sense pair, output fuse, ground strap, and ring-lug temperature rise if the regulation is unstable, because a poor sense circuit or ground return can force the converter to misread bus voltage while the power stage itself remains functional. Compare sense-wire voltage with stud voltage during the same load window so the fault boundary can land on one sense branch, one lug, or one fuse element. |
| T6 | Compare converter output current, if available in live data, with the actual low-voltage load request, and inspect the housing and cooling plate for thermal shutdown clues that would explain an output that starts correct and then falls away after several minutes. |
| T7 | Recheck the measurement directly at the converter after any loose lug, fuse, or ground issue is corrected, making sure the improved voltage is real at the battery terminals and not only at the converter stud. The release condition is restored regulation at both the converter and battery ends of the same current path under the same accessory load. |
| T8 | Sign off the output check only after the work order names the fault as converter internal regulation failure, high-resistance output stud, blown low-voltage fuse, poor ground strap, or remote-sense circuit issue, with the measured voltages and load condition written beside the named hardware. The final record should make clear which interface actually dropped voltage and which one remained normal during the loaded test. |
