| T1 | On Novaris ChargePort-379 Plus, define the complaint as AC charge abort after rain, charging unavailable after a vehicle wash, or intermittent control-pilot fault at plug insertion, and record the charger type, ambient humidity, charge-inlet door position, and whether the failure occurs before contactor close or only after the onboard charger begins current draw. |
| T2 | Disable the charging path, confirm the inlet is de-energized, and remove the charge-port bezel and inner splash shield to expose the Mennekes Type 2 inlet body, the TE MQS rear connector, the control-pilot and proximity-pilot pin cavities, the inlet drain channel, the body-to-inlet foam gasket, and the harness grommet passing into the quarter panel. |
| T3 | Inspect the face of the Type 2 inlet first, checking the CP and PP cavities, latch pocket, drain slots, and lower seal lip for standing water, white mineral trace, arc soot, or road grit packed tightly enough to hold moisture against the pilot pins after the external surface appears dry. |
| T4 | Open the rear side and inspect the TE MQS connector, secondary lock, rear seal, strain relief, and harness cloth wrap for capillary water tracks, green copper oxide, swollen seal lips, or evidence that the harness was tied in a low loop that channels water toward the connector instead of toward the body drain. |
| T5 | Perform continuity and pin-drag checks on the CP, PP, and inlet-lock circuits, then compare those readings dry and after a controlled water-path test directed at the bezel seam, the lower drain slots, and the harness grommet so the actual entry route is identified instead of inferred from corrosion alone. |
| T6 | If the fault appears only under water exposure, trace whether moisture enters through the face seal, the bezel gasket, the rear MQS seal, the grommet slit, or a blocked drain channel. If the branch stays dry but charging still aborts, separate pilot-circuit hardware faults from onboard-charger logic by comparing inlet measurements with the charger-side response. |
| T7 | Inspect the body flange and the inlet mounting screws for distortion or missing compression that could keep the foam gasket from sealing evenly, because a tilted inlet shell often creates a repeat water path even after pins and connectors have been cleaned or replaced. |
| T8 | Restore the inlet assembly and rerun the charging sequence with repeated plug insertions, charge-start attempts, and a final water-path confirmation so the acceptance result covers both the electrical pilot behavior and the physical sealing path around the inlet body. |
| T9 | Document the exact moisture boundary at cavity level, naming whether the issue belongs to the Type 2 face seal, drain-channel blockage, TE MQS rear connector, harness grommet, inlet mounting distortion, or another source upstream of the charger electronics. |
| T10 | Release the vehicle only after the closeout note ties the repaired or confirmed boundary to the measured CP and PP behavior, the controlled water-path result, and the final successful charge-start sequence, so the next technician is not left with a generic charge-port water ingress statement. |
