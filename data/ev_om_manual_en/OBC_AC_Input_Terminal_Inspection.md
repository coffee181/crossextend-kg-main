| T2 | Isolate the vehicle from the mains supply, make the high-voltage side safe, and remove the OBC AC-input cover so the L terminal, N terminal, protective-earth stud, ring lugs, ferrite, and cable gland can be seen without changing the original cable lay. |
| T3 | Read the as-found condition at each input stud, looking for darkened tin plating, blued nut witness, melted insulation sleeve, loose ring-lug clocking, or soot around the gland entry that would point to heat generation at one terminal rather than at the charger power stage. |

| T5 | Compare the suspect terminal with the other AC positions and with the PE stud, noting plating color, washer imprint, cable stiffness, and odor intensity, and inspect the stranded conductor at the crimp barrel for broken strands or heat-hardened insulation near the lug neck. |
| T6 | If the terminal damage is not obvious, reassemble enough for a monitored low-current charge attempt and watch for temperature rise at the exact L, N, or PE hardware using an infrared probe, so the inspection identifies the heating joint instead of assuming the whole AC input is faulty. |
| T7 | Release the inspection only after the final note names the precise interface as loose L terminal nut, overheated neutral ring lug, damaged PE stud washer stack, cable-gland strain, or no fault found at the AC entry, with each named part tied to observed evidence. |
