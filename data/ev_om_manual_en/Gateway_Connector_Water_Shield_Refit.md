| T4 | Unlatch XG14 and open the shield just enough to inspect the inner seal, CPA position, terminal cavity faces, and shield-drain crimp. Compare the left upper cavity area with the lower downstream side of the same connector, because moisture that starts at the cowl edge usually stains the upper seal first while a connector-back leak leaves the rear wire exit wet instead. |
| T5 | Measure backbone resistance at splice S214 with the network asleep, then measure wake-line leakage from the affected pin to the shield-drain eyelet before the area is dried. A healthy branch should stay near 60 ohms on CAN and above 1 megaohm to ground on the wake lead, while the failed branch in this condition usually shows reduced insulation or a drifting wake voltage that follows the moisture line. |
| T6 | Dry only the water-shield cavity and repeat the leakage check without moving the gateway module or the downstream splice. If the wake-line leakage recovers after the connector shield is dried while splice S214 and the downstream harness remain unchanged, isolate the fault boundary to the connector water shield, upper seal lip, or rear wire-exit support rather than the gateway itself. |
| T7 | Replace or reseat the connector water shield, restore the CPA, and route the harness branch through the original clip-to-bracket path so the shield is not twisted toward the rear. Confirm that the drain slit points downward, the upper lip is seated against the cowl flange, and the shield-drain eyelet is held flat under its stud instead of floating on the insulation sleeve. |
| T8 | Run a controlled water-path check from the upper cowl downward, then hold the vehicle through one sleep cycle and one wake cycle while monitoring sleep current, wake-line voltage, and gateway status. Release only after the shield area remains dry, sleep current stays within the expected band, and no fresh wake event is confirmed at XG14 or splice S214 after the spray test. |
| T9 | Reinstall the splash shield, kick panel, and cowl trim in the original order and document the final boundary clearly. The accepted closeout is moisture isolated to the connector water shield path and restored to normal with stable CAN resistance, stable wake-line insulation, and no renewed water track from upper cowl to lower harness pocket. |
