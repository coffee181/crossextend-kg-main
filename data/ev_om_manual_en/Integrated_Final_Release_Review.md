| T1 | On Aurex CrossFlow-747 EV, start the rear-loop release inspection by naming the repaired cooling hardware on the rear inverter loop, such as the molded manifold, the upper branch hose, the bleed screw, the outlet temperature sensor, or the quick-connect fitting at the inverter outlet. Record coolant specification, parts replaced, and the original leak or temperature complaint before the closeout run starts. |
| T2 | Expose the rear inverter manifold area and inspect it as one assembled path, including the molded manifold body, both quick-connect joints, the hose clamps, the bleed screw, the temperature-sensor connector, the nearby harness clip, and the undertray edge that shields the branch from road splash. Keep the upper outlet and lower return visible so upstream and downstream evidence are not mixed together. |
| T3 | Hold the loop at the specified pressure and verify that the manifold body, quick-connect seals, bleed-screw seat, and hose ends remain dry before circulation begins. Record the pressure value used and whether any wetting starts at the connector collar, the hose cut face, the bleed seat, or the manifold seam, because those origins lead to different repair boundaries. |
| T4 | Run the rear-loop pump service routine and watch pump current, reservoir response, return-flow stability, and the temperature difference across the inverter branch while checking the manifold and hose joints for seepage, fine bubble return, or clamp movement under flow. Compare the outlet-side behavior with the inlet side so a downstream restriction is not hidden behind a simple no-leak judgment. |
| T5 | Check the electrical side of the repair at the same time by confirming temperature-sensor connector latch, sensor plausibility, and harness support on the clip-to-bracket path. The loop is not ready for release if the coolant hardware is dry but the sensor branch is unsupported, disconnected, or resting on the manifold or rear cradle bracket. |
| T6 | Inspect routing and clearance with the undertray still off, verifying that the manifold hoses clear the rear cradle, half-shaft path, bracket edges, and adjacent low-voltage harness through the normal motion window. Pay attention to clamp-ear direction and connector-lock orientation, because a dry static branch can still chafe once the suspension and drive unit move under load. |
| T7 | Refit the undertray and local guards, then repeat the dry check and pump run long enough to cover the final supported shape of the hoses and the vibration state created by the panel stack. This second pass is the release boundary for the fully assembled rear loop, not just for the exposed manifold before closure. |
| T8 | Release the vehicle only after the work record names the final condition of the manifold body, seal points, bleed screw, sensor connector, hose route, pump behavior, and undertray-supported clearance together. Accept the review when the rear inverter loop is documented as dry, electrically connected, properly routed, and stable in both open and closed hardware states. |
