| Time step | O&M sample text |
|---|---|
| T1 | On Lynxar FusionDrive-316 AWD, identify the shop EVSE used for validation, such as a WallPort AC22 unit with a 32 A single-phase output, and record that the check is aimed at the same AC charging complaint reported by the customer. Note connector type, available current, ambient temperature, and battery state of charge before the inlet is touched. |
| T2 | Inspect the charge-inlet face and the rear inlet cavity before plugging in, covering the control-pilot pin, proximity-pilot resistor path, lock actuator, latch switch, inlet NTC, and rear connector C441. Look for bent CP plating, moisture stain at the lower drain slit, witness marks on the lock pawl, and harness twist between the inlet body and the rear support bracket. |
| T3 | Connect the shop charging cable and record insertion force, latch engagement, CP duty-cycle recognition, PP value, onboard-charger wake, and the first charger-enable transition. The validation should capture the full handshake chain from inlet face to OBC enable, not just whether the EVSE display eventually shows charging. |
| T4 | Hold the AC session long enough to observe current ramp, inlet temperature rise, latch stability, and whether the rear connector remains seated after the cable weight settles into its natural downward position. Compare the live CP and PP values with the expected values for the selected cable so a cable-side mismatch is not misread as a vehicle-side inlet defect. |
| T5 | If the session drops out, compare dropout timing with CP instability, PP fluctuation, latch-switch state, inlet NTC rise, and onboard-charger enable. This separates a weak pilot terminal, a false plug-present path, a lock-actuator issue, and a true OBC request problem, which can all stop the session while leaving very different evidence on the inlet assembly. |
| T6 | Repeat the charge start and stop sequence with the same EVSE, then if needed with a second known-good cable of the same rating, while keeping C441 and the latch path observable. The second pass is required because some repaired inlet faults only return after the first thermal expansion and unlatch cycle. |
| T7 | After charging ends, inspect the inlet face, rear connector, lock pawl, and drain path again for fresh witness marks, abnormal warmth, or partial latch return. Pay attention to whether the rear connector has rotated in its bracket or whether the latch-switch lever now sits in a different position than it did before load was applied. |
| T8 | Define the final boundary as CP terminal instability, PP resistor path error, lock-actuator or latch-switch fault, inlet-temperature rise, rear connector seating issue, or no fault found under the reproduced AC condition. A valid release requires the same EVSE condition to complete repeated handshakes without dropout and without new physical evidence at the inlet assembly. |
| T9 | Sign off only after the closeout note records EVSE identity, cable rating, repeated charge-start outcome, CP and PP behavior, inlet temperature result, and the post-test mechanical state of the inlet and rear connector. That record keeps the acceptance tied to one real charging condition rather than to a vague workshop charging check. |
