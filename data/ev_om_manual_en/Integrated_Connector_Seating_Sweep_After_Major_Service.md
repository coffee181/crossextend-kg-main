| T1 | On Helion FusionDrive-531 AWD, identify the exact connector being swept after service, such as the front inverter resolver plug C72, and state why it was disturbed during the repair. Record any related DTC, whether the fault is intermittent or constant, and whether the problem appears only when the harness is clipped into its final position on the upper inverter bracket. |
| T2 | Expose connector C72, its CPA, the silicone seal line, the terminal cavities, the first harness clip, and the guide rib on the inverter housing without pulling the branch away from its normal route. The connector must be judged in the installed position because a branch that looks correct in free air may be loaded sideways once it is held by the clip and bracket. |
| T3 | Inspect the outer housing for latch damage, incomplete CPA travel, rolled seal, cavity contamination, and witness polish where the branch passes the clip-to-bracket edge. Compare the housing clocking with the inverter guide rib and note whether the branch bends upward, rearward, or downward as soon as the clip is seated. |
| T4 | Unplug C72 and compare terminal height, drag feel, and seal compression across each cavity, then inspect the mating pins and guide surfaces on the inverter side. This is where a single backed-out terminal, a relaxed female contact, or a cracked guide rib can be distinguished from a connector that merely looked underseated from the outside. |
| T5 | Measure resolver reference voltage and shield continuity after reconnecting the plug loosely, then again with the branch restored to its final clipped position. If voltage or signal stability changes only when the harness is clipped back onto the bracket, isolate the fault boundary to terminal seating or harness-induced pull load at C72 instead of to the resolver itself. |
| T6 | Reseat or repair the affected connector hardware, restore the CPA fully, and route the branch through the original guide so the first bend begins after the clip. Confirm that the connector body is seated squarely against the mating face, the seal line is even, and the clip is holding cable weight rather than twisting the plug upward. |
| T7 | Repeat the electrical check and a controlled wiggle check with the connector fully clipped, then record whether the fault remains as terminal back-out, incomplete CPA seating, contamination, housing-key damage, harness pull load, or no defect found in connector seating. The accepted condition must describe both the cavity-level result and the installed final state. |
| T8 | Close the inspection only after the record states the connector identity, cavity findings, measured reference result, and final seating geometry in one chain from housing to clip to bracket, and after the accepted state confirms stable resolver reference and shield continuity with C72 fully seated and clipped in its final route. |
