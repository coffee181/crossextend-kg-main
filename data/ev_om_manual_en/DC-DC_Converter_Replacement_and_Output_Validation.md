| T1 | On Velorian DCDC-3K3, capture the original complaint, part number, serial number, and any related low-voltage DTCs before removal, because the replacement record is incomplete if the failed converter identity and the vehicle symptom are separated. Keep the intake note tied to the actual low-voltage operating state, such as wake sag, weak recovery after drive, or unstable cabin loads, so the replacement can later be judged against the same symptom window. |
| T2 | Isolate the traction battery and the 12 V battery, then remove the access panels so the converter housing, HV input connector, 12 V output stud, ground strap, remote-sense lead, LIN or CAN control connector, and coolant hoses are all visible in one as-found layout. |
| T3 | Before disconnecting anything, mark the washer order at the 12 V output stud, label the control connector and sense lead, and inspect the insulation boots and cable bends so any pre-existing cable strain or overheated lug condition is captured before the old unit is lifted out. |
| T4 | Disconnect the coolant hoses with plugs ready, then remove the low-voltage output cable, ground strap, sense lead, control plug, and HV input connector in controlled order, keeping spilled coolant away from the control connector cavities and low-voltage fuse hardware. |
| T5 | Support the converter, remove the mounting fasteners and sleeves, and lift the unit from the tray without dragging the cooling plate across the bracket edge. Once on the bench, compare the old and new units for port orientation, bracket boss depth, connector keying, and label revision before installation. |
| T6 | Transfer only the approved reusable parts, such as mounting sleeves, outer shields, or hose support clips, and replace any flattened coolant O-ring, heat-darkened ring lug, or damaged rubber isolator rather than carrying borderline hardware into the new assembly. |
| T7 | Install the replacement converter squarely, torque the tray fasteners and electrical studs to the specified sequence, reconnect the HV input and all low-voltage interfaces, and verify the output cable, sense lead, and ground strap leave the housing without side-loading the studs. Confirm that the ground strap and sense lead start their bend after the first support point rather than directly at the converter face, because stud-side pull is a common source of false post-repair undervoltage. |
| T8 | Refill and bleed the converter cooling branch, then power the vehicle and confirm that the converter wakes normally, reports on the network, shows stable coolant circulation, and develops no fresh seepage at the hose necks or cooling-plate perimeter while idling in READY. Record pump behavior, branch temperature rise, and the first minutes of wet or dry condition so the cooling proof is tied to an actual operating window and not just to a static fill result. |
| T9 | Validate low-voltage performance by measuring output at the converter stud and at the auxiliary battery during no-load and loaded conditions, making sure the regulation stays stable when blower, lighting, and pumps are switched on together. Compare the converter-side voltage, battery-side voltage, and any drop across the ground path so a good replacement unit is not falsely released with a remaining cable, stud, or strap issue downstream of the housing. |
| T10 | Close the replacement only after the final note lists removed and installed converter identifiers, transferred hardware, coolant bleed result, measured 12 V output values, load condition, and the final accepted hardware boundary as fully restored converter, corrected support hardware, or remaining downstream issue. The release state is a converter that stays dry, wakes on the network, and holds stable low-voltage support at both the output stud and the battery under the same demand that originally produced the complaint. |
