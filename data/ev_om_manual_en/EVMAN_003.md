| T1 | Start the HV output corrosion review on Helion E-Motion-536 Plus by noting whether corrosion was found during scheduled service or after a moisture complaint, and capture the as-found condition of the HV output boot, stud cover, cable tray, and surrounding pack wall before any hardware is loosened. |
| T2 | Isolate the pack, remove the HV output stud cover, and expose the copper cable lug, terminal nut, washer stack, plated stud thread, insulating boot, and stud shoulder without changing the cable angle more than necessary. Keep the cable angle and boot clocking readable so the later moisture-path check is tied to the true as-found geometry. |
| T3 | Inspect the corrosion pattern across the terminal nut flats, lug face, stud thread, washer stack, boot interior, and nearby insulator surface to determine whether the source is trapped water, road contamination, galvanic attack, or reduced clamp load. This pattern review should separate clamp-loss corrosion at the contact stack from moisture trapped under the boot or runoff entering from outside the pocket. |
| T4 | Remove the nut and washers in sequence, lay the hardware out by order, and examine the underside of the cable lug, the stud seating land, and the first thread turns for pitting, oxide buildup, contact shadowing, or heat tint beneath the contact face. |
| T5 | Measure contact resistance from the cable lug to the stud and compare it with the opposite-side output connection, taking care that the probe points land on clean metal and not on corroded thread, coated washer faces, or discolored nut surfaces. |
| T6 | Inspect the rubber boot lip, the drain path around the cover edge, the cable-entry orientation, and the nearby harness support to see whether water tracked down the cable, bypassed the boot seal, or sat trapped at the bottom of the stud pocket. |
| T7 | If the joint is under inspection only, reassemble the washer stack in the documented order and restore the insulating boot with the cable held at its natural exit angle. If pitting, thread damage, or heat history exceeds reuse limits, tag the exact stud, lug, washer, or nut for replacement and name that failed hardware explicitly in the record. |
| T8 | Close the stud pocket and restore the cover only after the record includes the corrosion location, corrosion severity, hardware order, moisture path assessment, and measured resistance comparison to the healthy reference output joint. The release note should still let the next technician identify the exact stud stack, corrosion source, and moisture path without reopening the pocket to rediscover the boundary. |
