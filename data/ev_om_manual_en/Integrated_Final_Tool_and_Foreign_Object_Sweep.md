| T1 | On Trion SystemSync-431 Plus, start the front e-axle final sweep by listing every area that was opened during the repair, including the inverter top cover, the DC-link pocket, the three-phase busbar recess, the low-voltage connector tray, and the coolant trough along the housing edge. |
| T2 | Remove only the access pieces required for the closeout check and inspect the open compartment with direct light, mirror, and borescope, covering the inverter cover channel, bolt wells, gasket groove, harness raceway, coolant pocket, and the blind cavity near the phase-bus exit where dropped hardware often hides. |
| T3 | Check for loose fasteners, washers, sealant beads, rag fibers, clip fragments, and desiccant or packaging material, then compare the found item count with the job tooling record so a missing bolt is not mistaken for an item that was never introduced into the compartment. |
| T4 | Inspect the gasket groove and cover land for rolled seal, broken plastic guide, trapped debris, or old sealant residue that could prevent the inverter cover from closing squarely even when all bolts are present and tightened. |
| T5 | Follow the coolant trough and drain recess around the housing edge, making sure no nut, clip, or silicone fragment remains in a low spot where it could block drainage, abrade a hose, or hide until vibration moves it into the fan or connector area later in service. |
| T6 | Check the low-voltage tray, HVA connector pockets, phase-bus recess, and sensor-branch clips for dropped items and for harness segments left outside their retainers, because a clean compartment still fails closeout if the branches are reassembled with one clip or divider omitted. |
| T7 | Compare the final bolt count, torque marks, and cover sequence with the repair map, then confirm that every tool, socket bit, inspection mirror, and temporary cap used in the e-axle compartment has been positively accounted for before the last cover goes back on. |
| T8 | Refit the remaining access pieces and perform one last visual pass through every remaining opening, checking that the gasket stays seated, no fastener backs out during closure, and the harnesses and hoses are not pushed into the inverter cover edge by the final panels. |
| T9 | Release the e-axle compartment only after the closeout note lists the cleared cavities, recovered item count if any, bolt-count confirmation, and FOD-free status of the inverter housing, and after the accepted state confirms clear drain recesses, seated gasket, retained harnesses, and no loose item anywhere from the coolant trough to the blind phase-bus cavity. |
