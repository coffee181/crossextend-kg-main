| Time step | O&M sample text |
|---|---|
| T1 | On Arcline OBC-11K, record the exact communication code, whether AC charging is otherwise possible, and whether the charger wakes when the cable is inserted, because a network DTC without a wake event points to a different fault boundary than a charger that wakes but drops off the bus. |
| T2 | Expose the OBC low-voltage connector and the local harness breakout, then inspect the 20-way housing, lever lock, twisted CAN pair, wake line, fused ignition feed, and ground pin for pin recession, water marks, loose retention, or prior repair splices near the charger bracket. |
| T3 | With the charger asleep, measure network resistance across CAN high and CAN low at the vehicle side and compare it with the expected terminated value, then repeat at the charger side if needed so the open or short is localized before modules are unplugged unnecessarily. |
| T4 | Wake the vehicle and backprobe CAN high, CAN low, wake command, and ignition feed at the OBC connector, confirming that the charger receives the correct power and wake prerequisites before its missing messages are blamed on the module itself. |
| T5 | If power and wake are present but communication stays absent, inspect connector drag, terminal tension, and twisted-pair integrity through the first clipped section of harness, because broken conductors near the strain-relief bend often pass visual inspection while failing under slight movement. |
| T6 | If communication returns during connector or harness movement, hold the harness in the failed position and identify the exact point that changes bus activity, then repair or tag that physical section instead of replacing the charger on an intermittent harness fault. |
| T7 | Finish the confirmation only after the record states whether the communication DTC was caused by missing ignition feed, missing wake signal, CAN high open, CAN low short to ground, poor terminal retention, or charger internal non-response, using the named connector pins where possible. |
