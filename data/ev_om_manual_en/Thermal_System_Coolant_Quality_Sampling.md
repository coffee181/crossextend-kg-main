| T1 | On Lynxar CoolNet-242 EV, identify the thermal loop being sampled, the approved sample point, the coolant specification expected on the vehicle, and the reason for sampling, such as suspected contamination, scheduled maintenance, mixed-fluid concern, or post-repair reuse decision. |
| T2 | Bring the loop to the required sampling condition, which may mean stabilized circulation, cooled static condition, or a short pump run, and verify that the sample is not taken immediately after topping off or bleeding when the fluid column is still stratified. |
| T3 | Clean the sample point externally and expose the hose joint, drain cock, sample port, or service tee together with the nearby clamp and support hardware so any external dirt is kept out of the bottle and any seepage evidence at the port is documented separately from the fluid condition. |
| T4 | Draw the sample into a clean labeled container only after the first small purge volume is discarded if required by the service method, so the captured fluid represents the working loop and not residue trapped in the nipple or drain passage. |
| T5 | Inspect the sample immediately for color shift, suspended particles, metallic sparkle, oil sheen, gel formation, or odor change, then compare it with unused reference coolant of the same specification so the assessment is grounded in a real standard rather than memory. |
| T6 | Measure the required quantitative properties such as refractive concentration, conductivity, pH, or freeze-protection point according to the platform method, and record the instrument used and the fluid temperature so the numbers remain interpretable after extraction. |
| T7 | If contamination is present, relate it back to likely hardware sources such as compressor oil intrusion, corrosion in an aluminum cold plate, sealant debris, mixed coolant types, or degraded hose liner, rather than closing the record at a generic bad-coolant statement. |
| T8 | Seal the sample point, verify the branch is dry and properly resecured, and close the record only after the note lists loop identity, sample location, observed appearance, measured properties, and the maintenance decision that follows from the result. |
