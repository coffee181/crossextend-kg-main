| T1 | On Arcline PortCheck-308 EV, begin the post-repair check by naming what was repaired at the charge port, such as inlet replacement, CP circuit repair, lock actuator replacement, or harness-retention correction, and keep the original complaint visible in the same record line so the functional test matches the repaired subsystem. |
| T2 | With the rear trim still open if practical, inspect final hardware state at the inlet face and rear cavity, confirming pin alignment, lock-pawl free movement, CP and PP connector seating, door-switch bracket position, and seal compression before the first plug insertion. |
| T3 | Insert the approved AC handle slowly and verify insertion force, latch feel, and full lock-pin travel, watching from the rear that the actuator, guide sleeve, and harness branches do not bind or shift as the handle reaches full seat. |
| T4 | Check live data or direct measurement for CP voltage state, PP recognition, door-switch state, and lock status before charge request, because the repaired port should show a coherent pre-charge signal set before power transfer begins. |
| T5 | Start an AC charge session and confirm pilot handshake, charger wake-up, contactor progression if available, and stable current draw, then monitor the rear cavity for abnormal pin temperature rise, connector movement, or seal disturbance during the first minutes. |
| T6 | If the repair involved DC terminals or moisture-related faults, run the applicable secondary validation such as lower-terminal insulation check, water path confirmation, or DC plug fit assessment, so the repaired branch is proven under its actual use case rather than only by AC insertion. |
| T7 | Pause after the first successful charge session and inspect the rear cavity again with the handle removed, checking that the lock pawl, CP and PP branches, inlet seal, and rear harness clips all return to their neutral positions instead of hanging up after thermal expansion or cable weight. |
| T8 | Complete at least two insertion-lock-charge-release cycles and verify emergency release, manual release access, and door closing behavior, making sure the repair has not corrected one function at the expense of another nearby mechanism. |
| T9 | Compare the second cycle with the first cycle for insertion force, pilot stability, lock timing, and temperature behavior so the closeout reflects repeatability and not one clean pass after handling. |
| T10 | Finish the post-repair check only after the note links repaired part, verified signals, handle fit, lock behavior, charge result, repeat-cycle outcome, and final visual condition in one chain, and only after the port completes repeated insertion, lock, charge, release, and door-close actions without renewed DTC, abnormal heat, or rear-cavity disturbance. |
