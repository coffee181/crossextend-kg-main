| Time step | O&M sample text |
|---|---|
| T1 | On Arcline ThermoAir-788 EV, begin the post-replacement commissioning record by identifying the installed compressor part number, compressor serial number, specified refrigerant mass, POE oil grade, and the reason the original unit was removed, because the validation path differs if the previous fault was seizure, winding insulation, internal leak, or communication loss. |
| T2 | Before starting the new unit, confirm that the recovered and recharged circuit passed vacuum hold, that both HNBR line seals were renewed at the suction and discharge joints, and that the service-port caps, condenser fan circuit, and battery chiller branch were restored so the compressor is not judged while the rest of the heat-pump loop is incomplete. |
| T3 | Remove the local shield and inspect the installed hardware as a complete assembly, exposing the HVA280-3P compressor power connector, the Sumitomo TS090-8P control plug, the low-side pressure transducer, the high-side pressure transducer, the suction muffler bracket, the discharge line support, and the four compressor mount fasteners with their paint marks intact. |
| T4 | Verify that the transport caps were fully removed, the suction and discharge unions are seated squarely, the line brackets are not pulling the shell off center, and the harness has enough slack at the TS090-8P backshell to survive compressor rocking without loading the terminals or rear seal. |
| T5 | Power the thermal controller, check that insulation monitoring passes, and confirm that compressor wake, inverter enable, and speed command are present before the first rotation request, because a replacement unit can be mechanically perfect and still fail validation if the control branch or interlock path was disturbed during installation. |
| T6 | Start the compressor at low speed and record high-side pressure rise, low-side pull-down, current draw, shell vibration, and condenser-fan response during the first minute, paying attention to whether the suction line cools smoothly or shows hunting behavior that would indicate trapped air, incorrect charge, or an expansion-device issue outside the compressor itself. |
| T7 | Increase compressor speed through the mid-range operating band used during normal cabin cooling and battery chilling, then compare the pressure split, line temperature change, and noise signature at each plateau so the validation covers more than the idle-speed condition often used in a short bay check. |
| T8 | Inspect the mechanical side under load by watching the lower mounts, the discharge pipe clamp, the muffler bracket, and the HVA280 connector body for oscillation, wire whip, or bracket resonance, because a quiet new compressor can still fail later if the surrounding supports were preloaded during reassembly. |
| T9 | Shut the unit down and carry out an immediate leak and retention check at the suction joint, discharge joint, service ports, pressure sensors, and compressor body seam, using both visual evidence and residual pressure behavior so a slow leak is not missed after the dynamic run. |
| T10 | Release the vehicle only after the commissioning note states whether the final boundary is a successful compressor installation or a remaining issue in charge quantity, line support, connector retention, pressure sensing, expansion-device control, or another thermal-loop component, and ties that conclusion to the measured pressures, currents, and vibration observations from this run. |
