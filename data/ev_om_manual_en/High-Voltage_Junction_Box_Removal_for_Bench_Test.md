| Time step | O&M sample text |
|---|---|
| T1 | Prepare Velorian HVX-603 EV for junction-box bench removal by confirming the reason for the removal and documenting every connected branch before disassembly, including the main HV cables, low-voltage control plugs, HVIL branch, mounting fastener positions, and the as-found cable-support geometry around the housing. This intake map has to preserve both circuit identity and physical route so a bench-found issue is not later confused with a vehicle-side installation condition. |
| T2 | Isolate the high-voltage system, verify safe discharge, and label each cable lug, feedback connector, coil connector, interlock lead, and low-voltage branch by physical position and circuit identity before any fastener is loosened. Keep the label set tied to front or rear and left or right positions on the housing so the box can return to the same cable topology after bench work. |
| T3 | Remove the cover and protective barriers, then disconnect the low-voltage connectors and the HVIL branch first, protecting the terminal faces and locking features so the control-side condition is preserved for the planned bench evaluation. Record latch condition, seal condition, and pin alignment as each connector comes free so any bench diagnosis still knows whether the problem may already have been present at the vehicle-side interface. |
| T4 | Disconnect the high-voltage cable lugs in the documented order, cap the exposed studs, and keep the nut-and-washer stacks indexed by terminal so the original hardware order and cable positions can be reconstructed on the bench or during reinstallation. Maintain each stack with its terminal identity because washer order, lug orientation, and stud assignment are part of the electrical boundary and not just loose hardware. |
| T5 | Support the junction-box body, remove the mounting fasteners, and lift the unit clear without dragging the vehicle-side harnesses, making sure the detached branches are left strain-free and the exposed vehicle-side terminals remain fully insulated and labeled. Watch the harness exits and bracket edges during lift-out so a trapped section or hidden side load is identified on the vehicle before the box leaves the shelf. |
| T6 | Inspect the vehicle-side mounting shelf, cable routing, and isolated studs after removal so no hidden bracket damage, trapped harness section, or misplaced insulator is carried forward and later misread as a bench-found defect. The removal boundary should separate any unit-internal fault from a vehicle-side routing, bracket, or terminal-protection issue before the unit reaches the bench. |
| T7 | Transfer the removed unit to the bench only after the work order lists cable identity, connector identity, hardware order, isolation protection, and the physical condition of every disconnected vehicle-side terminal and harness branch. The accepted handoff is a bench-bound unit with all upstream branches preserved, protected, and documented well enough that reinstallation can restore the same electrical and mechanical path without guesswork. |
