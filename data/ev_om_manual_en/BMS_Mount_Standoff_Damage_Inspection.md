| T1 | On Velorian E-Core-421 Plus, define the BMS complaint as intermittent cell-voltage dropout, repeated sense-board communication fault, or a vibration-sensitive fault that appears after battery-pack handling, and note whether the enclosure was recently opened for module or harness service. |
| T2 | Isolate the BMS enclosure and expose the cell-monitoring board, the nylon mounting standoffs, the M4 board screws, the sense-ribbon connector, and the foam anti-vibration pad so the board support path can be inspected before the board is flexed. |
| T3 | Inspect each mounting standoff for cracking, pulled threads, missing insulating shoulder, uneven board gap, or witness marks showing the PCB has been rocking against the enclosure instead of being supported at all designated points. |
| T4 | Check whether the sense-ribbon connector and nearby solder joints change state when light pressure is applied near the suspect standoff, because a broken support pillar often causes an electrical complaint only when the PCB bends under vibration or cover preload. |
| T5 | Compare the suspect standoff height and screw engagement with the adjacent supports, making sure the board is not being clamped on one corner while another corner floats above the pad or the insulating washer stack. |
| T6 | Finish the inspection only after the fault is reduced to cracked nylon standoff, missing shoulder washer, stripped M4 board screw, collapsed foam support, connector disturbance secondary to board flex, or no support defect found on the monitored board, and after any corrected board support leaves the PCB evenly seated on its standoffs and pad with no renewed signal change when the same light pressure is applied near the suspect corner. |
