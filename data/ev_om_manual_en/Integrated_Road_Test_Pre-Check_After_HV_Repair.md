| Time step | O&M sample text |
|---|---|
| T1 | On Novaris FusionDrive-406 EV, start the road-test pre-check by recording what was just repaired on the front drive unit, which DTCs were previously active, and whether the vehicle already reaches ready state without fresh inverter or thermal faults. Note battery state of charge, front inverter coolant temperature, and the exact reason the road test is being requested so later release evidence matches the repaired boundary. |
| T2 | Open the front drive-unit service area and expose the inverter phase connector cover, resolver connector JR2, the coolant inlet hose, the coolant outlet hose, the bleed screw on the upper manifold, and the harness P-clip mounted on the left motor-mount bracket. Keep both inlet and outlet paths visible from upper manifold to lower pump return so leak, rub, and seating evidence can be judged in one pass. |
| T3 | Inspect the as-serviced condition before powering the vehicle, checking connector latch position, cover fastener seating, hose-clamp clocking, bleed-screw witness mark, and whether the harness branch is routed through the bracket clip instead of resting on the coolant outlet neck. A missing clip or rotated clamp can pass a static visual check yet fail as soon as pump flow and drive-unit vibration begin. |
| T4 | Reconnect low-voltage power, command ready state, and confirm that the inverter low-voltage feed, resolver reference, and HVIL status recover without delay. Record the low-voltage supply at the inverter, the resolver reference value at JR2, and whether any new interlock or resolver-correlation fault appears before wheels or shafts are moved. |
| T5 | Command the drive-unit coolant pump and monitor inlet temperature, outlet temperature, and pump current while the vehicle remains stationary. A stable pre-road-test condition should show clear flow from inlet to outlet, a repeatable pump current in the expected band, and no seepage at the upper bleed screw, the lower hose clamp, or the manifold-to-housing joint. |
| T6 | Hold ready state long enough for the front inverter to exchange heat with the loop, then compare the outlet temperature rise and pump behavior with the inlet side. If outlet temperature climbs rapidly while pump current remains normal and the upper hose still feels soft, isolate the fault boundary toward trapped air or an outlet-side restriction rather than toward the inverter electronics or the inlet supply hose. |
| T7 | With the area still open, apply a light torque request on the lift or in service mode and watch the phase connector cover, JR2 backshell, and harness P-clip while the drive unit reacts. The harness must stay held by the bracket clip, the connector locks must not lift, and no fresh coolant trace should appear as the unit rolls slightly against its mounts. |
| T8 | If any abnormality is seen, correct it before the road test and rerun the same stationary sequence from ready state through pump command and light torque request. The road test is not released on a partial improvement, because a stable ready state without stable coolant flow or connector retention still leaves the true repaired boundary unconfirmed. |
| T9 | Close only the nonessential trim, then perform one final stationary pre-check with the disturbed hardware still observable, confirming no fresh DTC, no leak at the upper or lower hose path, and no harness movement at the clip-to-bracket location. This step proves the issue is either repaired at the serviced front drive-unit boundary or still present before road load adds new variables. |
| T10 | Approve the vehicle for road test only after the record states the exact release boundary in plain hardware terms, such as stable inverter connector retention, stable resolver signal, normal pump current, and no coolant loss from inlet, outlet, or bleed points. If any one of those conditions is not met, hold the vehicle and keep the fault assigned to the serviced front drive-unit interface instead of passing it downstream to road evaluation. |
