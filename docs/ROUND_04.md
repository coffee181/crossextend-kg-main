# Round 4

Round 4 moved the pipeline from the three-document mini-regression to the full 9-document human-gold benchmark, while preserving the explicit CNC alignment policy discovered in Round 3. The mainline fixes were deliberately narrow: runtime label normalization in [pipeline/evidence.py](D:/crossextend_kg/pipeline/evidence.py), stronger verification-fragment rejection and better observation/fault anchor hints in [rules/filtering.py](D:/crossextend_kg/rules/filtering.py), and a reproducible round-execution layer through [experiments/rounds.py](D:/crossextend_kg/experiments/rounds.py), [scripts/prepare_round_run.py](D:/crossextend_kg/scripts/prepare_round_run.py), and [scripts/evaluate_variant_run.py](D:/crossextend_kg/scripts/evaluate_variant_run.py).

The post-fix rerun improved macro concept F1 from `0.6559` to `0.6818`, anchor accuracy from `0.9347` to `0.9410`, and relation F1 from `0.4701` to `0.5049`, while shrinking predictions from `425/195` concepts/relations to `415/179`. The ablation also made the paper story clearer: rule filtering matters, but the current 9-document corpus shows only marginal gains for memory-bank and LLM-attachment variants. The biggest remaining risk is still data quality rather than pipeline logic, especially [battery_BATOM_001.json](D:/crossextend_kg/data/ground_truth/battery_BATOM_001.json), whose gold steps do not fully match the staged document.
